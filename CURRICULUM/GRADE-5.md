LEARNING OUTCOMES

Bible / Covenant Studies

Student can explain:
God gives instruction.
Law teaches order and responsibility.
Justice matters to covenant life.
Obedience is connected to love and wisdom.
Worship shapes community identity.
God’s law guides how people treat one another.

Reading

Read grade-level literature and informational texts.
Identify theme, main idea, and supporting evidence.
Compare characters, settings, and events.
Summarize longer passages.
Analyze vocabulary using context clues.
Identify point of view.
Cite text evidence in written responses.

Writing

Write multi-paragraph essays.
Write narratives with clear structure.
Write explanatory responses.
Use evidence from reading.
Use transitions.
Revise and edit for clarity.
Write law, justice, and responsibility reflections.

Mathematics

Add, subtract, multiply, and divide multi-digit numbers.
Add and subtract fractions with unlike denominators.
Multiply fractions.
Understand decimals to hundredths.
Convert measurement units.
Graph points on a coordinate plane.
Solve multi-step word problems.
Interpret data.

Science

Describe matter and energy.
Understand ecosystems and food chains.
Study Earth systems.
Observe weather and climate patterns.
Understand basic physical science concepts.
Plan simple investigations.
Use evidence to support explanations.

Social Studies

Understand laws and civic responsibility.
Study early civilizations.
Identify how communities create order.
Read maps, timelines, and primary-source-style texts.
Compare government structures.
Discuss justice and responsibility.
Connect law to community flourishing.

Spanish

Use greetings, introductions, and classroom phrases.
Count 1-100 in Spanish.
Use family, school, community, law, and justice vocabulary.
Read short Spanish paragraphs.
Answer oral questions.
Write simple Spanish sentences.
Use Bible vocabulary in Spanish.
Describe rules, family, and community in Spanish.

AI / Digital Literacy

Use AI tools only with adult guidance.
Create simple prompts.
Compare AI answers with trusted sources.
Understand digital responsibility.
Protect private information.
Identify possible misinformation.
Use technology to create a simple project.
Explain that tools must serve truth and learning.

Art

Create art connected to law, order, and community.
Use color, line, shape, pattern, and texture.
Create a visual symbol of justice or responsibility.
Explain artistic choices in writing or speech.

Music

Sing songs connected to memory and worship.
Recognize rhythm, tempo, melody, and repetition.
Participate in group music.
Use Spanish vocabulary in songs.
Connect music to law, memory, and community.

Health / Wellness

Practice healthy routines.
Understand nutrition, exercise, and rest.
Identify emotions and self-control strategies.
Practice safe movement.
Discuss responsibility for body and mind.
Ask trusted adults for help.

Service Learning

Serve a family or community need.
Create a responsibility project.
Help younger students or siblings.
Write a thank-you or encouragement note.
Pray for leaders, families, and communities.
Reflect on justice, responsibility, and service.
