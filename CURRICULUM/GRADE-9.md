LEARNING OUTCOMES

Bible / Covenant Studies

Student can explain:
Creation establishes order and purpose.
Human beings are called to stewardship.
Identity begins with God as Creator.
Worship is a response to creation.
Truth is not invented but received.
Responsibility flows from covenant purpose.

English / Literature

Read grade-level literature and nonfiction.
Analyze theme, structure, and character development.
Identify symbolism and author purpose.
Compare texts across cultures and time periods.
Use textual evidence in analysis.
Discuss creation, calling, identity, and responsibility in literature.

Writing

Write organized essays.
Develop clear thesis statements.
Use evidence and explanation.
Write narrative, explanatory, and argumentative pieces.
Revise for structure and clarity.
Edit for grammar, mechanics, and style.
Practice MLA-style citation foundations.

Mathematics

Understand algebraic expressions.
Solve linear equations and inequalities.
Graph linear functions.
Understand slope and intercepts.
Solve systems of equations.
Apply exponent rules.
Interpret data.
Use algebra to model real-world problems.

Science

Understand cell structure and function.
Study genetics and heredity.
Understand ecosystems and energy flow.
Study biological classification.
Conduct lab-style investigations.
Use evidence to support scientific explanations.
Connect biological systems to order and stewardship.

Social Studies

Study early world civilizations.
Analyze geography, culture, religion, and government.
Read maps, timelines, and source excerpts.
Understand the development of societies.
Compare civilizations and belief systems.
Discuss stewardship, order, and responsibility in history.

Spanish

Use Spanish I vocabulary and grammar foundations.
Introduce self and others.
Discuss family, school, home, and faith.
Use present-tense verbs.
Read short Spanish passages.
Write simple Spanish paragraphs.
Participate in basic conversations.
Use Spanish Bible and worship vocabulary.

AI / Digital Literacy

Understand what AI systems are.
Use AI tools with accountability and citation.
Create effective prompts.
Evaluate AI output for accuracy and bias.
Protect private information.
Understand academic integrity with AI.
Use digital tools for research and portfolio development.
Explain technology as a tool, not an identity.

Art / Media

Create visual work connected to creation and stewardship.
Use design elements intentionally.
Analyze media messages.
Produce portfolio-ready creative work.

Music / Worship

Study music as memory, worship, and culture.
Identify rhythm, melody, theme, and repetition.
Connect music to spiritual formation and cultural identity.
Participate in music or worship reflection.

Health / Wellness

Understand physical health routines.
Discuss emotional regulation and stress.
Understand digital wellness.
Practice responsible decision-making.
Build habits of rest, movement, and focus.

Service Learning

Complete a stewardship project.
Serve family, church, or community.
Document service hours.
Reflect on responsibility and creation care.
Connect service to covenant purpose.
