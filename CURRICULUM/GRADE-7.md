LEARNING OUTCOMES

Bible / Covenant Studies

Student can explain:
Scripture is preserved across generations.
Truth must be remembered and studied.
Translation helps people receive the Word.
Faithful communities protect sacred teaching.
Wisdom requires careful reading.
The Word shapes worship, ethics, and identity.

Reading

Read grade-level literature and informational texts.
Analyze theme and central idea.
Compare texts across historical contexts.
Identify author purpose and perspective.
Evaluate evidence in a passage.
Summarize complex texts.
Cite textual evidence accurately.

Writing

Write structured arguments.
Write explanatory essays.
Use claims, reasons, and evidence.
Organize paragraphs with transitions.
Revise for clarity and logic.
Edit for grammar and mechanics.
Write reflections on Scripture, memory, and truth.

Mathematics

Apply ratios and proportional relationships.
Work with rational numbers.
Solve equations and inequalities.
Analyze scale drawings.
Use percentages in real-world problems.
Understand probability foundations.
Interpret data and graphs.
Apply mathematical reasoning to systems.

Science

Study matter and energy.
Understand physical and life science systems.
Explain cycles and interactions.
Conduct investigations.
Use evidence to support claims.
Analyze patterns in data.
Connect scientific observation to ordered systems.

Social Studies

Study world history and civilizations.
Understand how texts shape cultures.
Analyze traditions, laws, and institutions.
Use maps, timelines, and source excerpts.
Compare historical perspectives.
Discuss preservation of knowledge.
Connect literacy to civilization.

Spanish

Use greetings, introductions, and classroom phrases.
Count beyond 200 in Spanish.
Use vocabulary for Scripture, study, family, community, and history.
Read short Spanish paragraphs.
Answer oral questions in complete phrases.
Write short Spanish sentences.
Use Spanish Bible vocabulary.
Describe school, study habits, and family in Spanish.

AI / Digital Literacy

Use AI tools with adult guidance.
Create clear prompts for research support.
Evaluate AI responses against trusted sources.
Identify misinformation and weak evidence.
Understand source credibility.
Protect personal information.
Practice responsible digital communication.
Use technology to support learning and portfolio work.

Art

Create art connected to Scripture or preservation.
Use visual symbols to communicate ideas.
Apply design elements intentionally.
Explain artistic choices in writing or speech.

Music

Recognize rhythm, melody, tempo, and repetition.
Participate in group music.
Use Spanish vocabulary in songs.
Connect music to memory, worship, and preservation.

Health / Wellness

Understand healthy routines.
Discuss stress, rest, and focus.
Practice emotional self-awareness.
Understand digital wellness.
Practice safe movement.
Ask trusted adults for help.

Service Learning

Help preserve family or community memory.
Interview a family or community member.
Create a thank-you or encouragement project.
Serve a family or community need.
Pray for wisdom and understanding.
Document service evidence.
