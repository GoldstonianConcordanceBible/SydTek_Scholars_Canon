LEARNING OUTCOMES

Bible / Covenant Studies

Student can explain:
God prepares people and nations.
Prophecy points toward fulfillment.
Waiting can form wisdom and discipline.
History reveals patterns of preparation.
Faithfulness matters before fulfillment arrives.
The covenant story prepares the way for Christ.

Reading

Read grade-level literature and informational texts.
Analyze theme, central idea, and structure.
Evaluate author purpose and perspective.
Compare texts across traditions and periods.
Use textual evidence to support analysis.
Summarize complex texts accurately.
Evaluate claims and evidence in nonfiction.

Writing

Write structured arguments.
Write explanatory essays.
Use claims, reasons, counterpoints, and evidence.
Use transitions and academic organization.
Revise for logic and clarity.
Edit for grammar, mechanics, and style.
Write preparation, prophecy, and fulfillment reflections.

Mathematics

Solve linear equations.
Understand functions.
Work with irrational numbers.
Apply the Pythagorean theorem.
Analyze data and scatter plots.
Understand slope and graphing foundations.
Use exponents and scientific notation.
Solve real-world mathematical problems.

Science

Study forces and motion.
Understand energy transfer.
Study life systems and heredity foundations.
Understand Earth systems and natural processes.
Conduct investigations.
Analyze data and evidence.
Explain patterns and cause-effect relationships.

Social Studies

Study world history and historical transition.
Analyze civilizations, empires, and reform movements.
Read maps, timelines, and source excerpts.
Evaluate historical cause and effect.
Compare cultures and institutions.
Discuss preparation, prophecy, and historical change.
Connect civic responsibility to wisdom and justice.

Spanish

Use greetings, introductions, and classroom phrases with confidence.
Use vocabulary for history, prophecy, school, family, and worship.
Read short Spanish paragraphs.
Answer oral questions in complete phrases.
Write short Spanish paragraphs.
Use Spanish Bible vocabulary.
Describe routines, study, family, and hope in Spanish.
Present a short Spanish introduction or reflection.

AI / Digital Literacy

Use AI tools with adult guidance.
Create research prompts.
Evaluate AI output against trusted sources.
Identify misinformation, weak evidence, and bias.
Understand digital judgment and source credibility.
Protect personal information.
Use technology to create portfolio evidence.
Explain how AI can assist but not replace thinking.

Art

Create art connected to preparation, prophecy, or hope.
Use symbols to communicate meaning.
Apply design elements intentionally.
Explain artistic choices in writing or speech.

Music

Recognize rhythm, melody, tempo, and repetition.
Participate in group music.
Use Spanish vocabulary in songs.
Connect music to worship, memory, and hope.

Health / Wellness

Understand healthy routines.
Discuss stress, rest, focus, and discipline.
Practice emotional self-awareness.
Understand digital wellness.
Practice safe movement.
Ask trusted adults for help.

Service Learning

Complete a preparation-focused service project.
Help a younger student or family member.
Create a hope or encouragement project.
Serve a family or community need.
Pray for wisdom and patience.
Document service evidence.
