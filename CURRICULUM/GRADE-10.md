LEARNING OUTCOMES

Bible / Covenant Studies

Student can explain:
Israel’s covenant identity.
The role of law in covenant life.
The importance of worship and memory.
The meaning of exile and return.
Justice and mercy in the covenant story.
How Israel’s story prepares later fulfillment.

English / Literature

Read grade-level literature and nonfiction.
Analyze themes of exile, return, identity, and justice.
Compare literary and historical texts.
Evaluate character, setting, conflict, and resolution.
Use textual evidence in analysis.
Connect literature to memory, culture, and responsibility.

Writing

Write literary analysis essays.
Write argumentative essays.
Write explanatory essays.
Use claims, evidence, and reasoning.
Use citations and source integration.
Revise for clarity, organization, and style.
Practice formal academic writing.

Mathematics

Understand geometric reasoning.
Use definitions, theorems, and proofs.
Analyze triangles, circles, and polygons.
Understand congruence and similarity.
Use coordinate geometry.
Apply right-triangle relationships.
Calculate area, surface area, and volume.
Solve real-world geometry problems.

Science

Understand atomic structure.
Study chemical bonding.
Understand matter and chemical reactions.
Use the periodic table.
Balance simple chemical equations.
Conduct lab-style investigations.
Use data to support scientific claims.
Connect order and change in physical systems.

Social Studies

Study world history from empires to modern transitions.
Analyze nations, migration, conflict, and reform.
Use maps, timelines, and primary-source-style texts.
Compare political and religious systems.
Discuss exile, identity, justice, and return.
Explain how civilizations remember and rebuild.

Spanish

Use Spanish II vocabulary and grammar.
Discuss community, worship, school, history, and identity.
Use present and past-tense foundations.
Read short Spanish passages.
Write short Spanish paragraphs.
Participate in guided conversation.
Use Spanish Bible and worship vocabulary.
Describe community responsibility in Spanish.

AI / Digital Literacy

Use AI tools with citation and accountability.
Create research prompts.
Evaluate AI output for bias and accuracy.
Compare AI responses with trusted sources.
Understand academic integrity.
Protect private information.
Use digital tools for presentations and portfolios.
Explain ethical technology use.

Art / Media

Create visual work connected to identity, memory, or return.
Analyze media narratives.
Use design elements intentionally.
Produce portfolio-ready creative work.

Music / Worship

Study music as worship, memory, and community identity.
Analyze rhythm, theme, repetition, and cultural meaning.
Participate in worship or music reflection.
Connect music to exile, return, and hope.

Health / Wellness

Practice healthy routines.
Understand stress, rest, focus, and resilience.
Discuss identity and responsibility.
Practice digital wellness.
Build habits of discipline and self-control.

Service Learning

Complete a community responsibility project.
Serve family, church, or community.
Document service hours.
Reflect on justice, mercy, and responsibility.
Connect service to covenant identity.
