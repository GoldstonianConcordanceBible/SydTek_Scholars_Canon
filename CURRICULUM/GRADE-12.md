LEARNING OUTCOMES

Bible / Covenant Studies

Student can explain:
The Kingdom calls people to faithful service.
Leadership requires humility and responsibility.
Stewardship includes gifts, time, knowledge, and resources.
Mission flows from covenant identity.
Wisdom guides public and private life.
Legacy is built through obedience, service, and truth.

English / Literature

Read advanced literature and nonfiction.
Analyze themes of mission, leadership, legacy, and responsibility.
Evaluate author purpose, structure, symbolism, and rhetoric.
Compare texts across cultures and historical periods.
Use textual evidence in advanced analysis.
Connect literature to calling, service, and human purpose.

Writing

Write college-ready analytical essays.
Write argumentative and explanatory essays.
Use thesis, claims, evidence, reasoning, and citations.
Integrate multiple sources responsibly.
Revise for logic, style, tone, and clarity.
Prepare capstone writing.
Prepare college, scholarship, or career writing samples.

Mathematics

Analyze advanced functions.
Work with polynomial, exponential, logarithmic, and trigonometric models.
Interpret graphs and transformations.
Solve real-world modeling problems.
Analyze data and rates of change.
Use mathematical reasoning for systems thinking.
Prepare for college-level quantitative work.

Science

Understand environmental systems.
Study ecology, resources, climate, and stewardship.
Analyze human impact on creation.
Conduct research-based investigations.
Use data to support claims.
Evaluate scientific arguments.
Connect science to responsibility and stewardship.

Social Studies

Understand government structures.
Understand economic systems.
Analyze citizenship and public responsibility.
Evaluate laws, policy, markets, and civic participation.
Discuss stewardship of resources.
Connect leadership and service to public life.
Prepare for informed adult citizenship.

Spanish

Use Spanish IV vocabulary and grammar.
Discuss mission, service, culture, leadership, and global communication.
Read intermediate Spanish passages.
Write structured Spanish paragraphs and reflections.
Participate in guided Spanish conversations.
Use past, present, and future tense structures.
Use Spanish Bible, service, and leadership vocabulary.
Prepare a Spanish capstone presentation.

AI / Digital Literacy

Use AI tools with academic integrity and citation.
Develop advanced prompts for research and planning.
Evaluate AI output for accuracy, bias, and ethical risk.
Use source verification.
Understand automation, systems, and leadership.
Discuss AI and human calling.
Create a responsible AI-use artifact.
Use digital tools for capstone portfolio development.

Art / Media

Create visual or media work connected to mission or legacy.
Analyze media messages and persuasion.
Use design elements intentionally.
Produce portfolio-ready creative work.
Create a capstone visual artifact.

Music / Worship

Study music as worship, memory, culture, and mission.
Analyze rhythm, theme, repetition, and cultural meaning.
Participate in worship or music reflection.
Connect music to mission, kingdom identity, and legacy.

Health / Wellness

Practice healthy adult routines.
Understand stress, discipline, resilience, and responsibility.
Discuss identity, vocation, service, and wise decisions.
Practice digital wellness.
Build habits for postsecondary life.

Service Learning

Complete a senior service project.
Serve family, church, school, or community.
Document service hours.
Reflect on leadership, stewardship, and mission.
Connect service to kingdom calling and legacy.
