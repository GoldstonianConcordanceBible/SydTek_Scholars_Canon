LEARNING OUTCOMES

Bible / Covenant Studies

Student can explain:
Christ fulfills covenant promise.
Christ fulfills law and prophecy.
Mercy and justice meet in Christ.
Discipleship requires obedience and love.
The New Covenant forms a redeemed people.
Kingdom identity shapes worship, ethics, and mission.

English / Literature

Read grade-level literature and nonfiction.
Analyze themes of redemption, mercy, sacrifice, and identity.
Compare texts across historical and cultural contexts.
Evaluate symbolism, structure, and author purpose.
Use textual evidence in advanced literary analysis.
Connect literature to moral responsibility and human transformation.

Writing

Write advanced literary analysis.
Write argumentative and explanatory essays.
Use thesis, claims, evidence, reasoning, and citations.
Integrate sources responsibly.
Revise for logic, style, and clarity.
Edit for grammar, mechanics, and academic tone.
Prepare college-ready writing samples.

Mathematics

Analyze functions and graphs.
Solve quadratic equations.
Work with polynomial expressions.
Understand exponential and logarithmic foundations.
Solve systems of equations.
Model real-world problems.
Interpret data and statistics.
Use algebraic reasoning for advanced problem solving.

Science

Understand motion, force, and energy.
Study waves and sound.
Understand electricity and magnetism foundations.
Conduct lab-style investigations.
Use mathematical models in science.
Analyze data and evidence.
Explain order, cause, and physical systems.

Social Studies

Study United States history.
Analyze liberty, law, responsibility, and citizenship.
Use primary-source-style documents.
Evaluate historical arguments.
Discuss civic responsibility and justice.
Connect national memory to moral responsibility.
Prepare for informed civic participation.

Spanish

Use Spanish III vocabulary and grammar.
Discuss faith, culture, history, community, and service.
Read intermediate Spanish passages.
Write structured Spanish paragraphs.
Participate in guided Spanish conversation.
Use past, present, and future tense foundations.
Use Spanish Bible and worship vocabulary.
Prepare a short Spanish presentation.

AI / Digital Literacy

Use AI tools with academic integrity.
Develop advanced prompts.
Evaluate AI output for accuracy, bias, and ethical risk.
Use citations and source verification.
Understand algorithmic influence.
Discuss AI, power, truth, and responsibility.
Use digital tools for research and portfolio development.
Prepare ethical AI-use reflections.

Art / Media

Create visual or media work connected to redemption or mercy.
Analyze media messages and persuasion.
Use design elements intentionally.
Produce portfolio-ready creative work.

Music / Worship

Study music as worship, memory, and theological expression.
Analyze rhythm, theme, repetition, and cultural meaning.
Participate in worship or music reflection.
Connect music to redemption, mercy, and kingdom identity.

Health / Wellness

Practice healthy routines.
Understand stress, discipline, and resilience.
Discuss identity, responsibility, and wise decisions.
Practice digital wellness.
Build habits of service, rest, and focus.

Service Learning

Complete a mercy or service project.
Serve family, church, or community.
Document service hours.
Reflect on redemption, responsibility, and compassion.
Connect service to discipleship and covenant identity.
