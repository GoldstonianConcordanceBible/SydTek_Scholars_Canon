**Applies To:** SydTek University (Primary), SydTek Scholars Alpha School (Limited Applications)

# Purpose

The Prior Learning Assessment (PLA) Framework establishes the institutional process for recognizing knowledge, skills, and competencies acquired outside traditional classroom instruction.

The purpose of PLA is to:

- reduce unnecessary educational cost;
- eliminate duplicate learning;
- recognize legitimate prior achievement;
- accelerate qualified learners;
- maintain academic integrity;
- preserve accreditation readiness;
- improve learner completion rates.

Recognition of prior learning does not reduce academic standards.

Every learner must demonstrate the same competency expectations required of traditionally enrolled students.

---

# Institutional Philosophy

Learning occurs in many environments.

Knowledge may be acquired through:

- employment;
- military service;
- entrepreneurship;
- independent study;
- previous college coursework;
- industry certifications;
- apprenticeships;
- volunteer service;
- research;
- professional practice.

The institution recognizes demonstrated learning—not simply experience.

Experience alone does not automatically receive academic recognition.

---

# Guiding Principles

Prior Learning Assessment must be:

- evidence based;
- academically rigorous;
- consistently applied;
- transparent;
- faculty governed;
- legally compliant;
- accreditation aligned;
- learner centered.

Every decision must be documented.

# Eligible Sources of Prior Learning

PLA may evaluate learning obtained through:

## Previous Academic Coursework

Examples include:

- accredited colleges;
- universities;
- international institutions;
- approved transfer partners.

---

## Professional Experience

Examples include:

- management;
- consulting;
- engineering;
- healthcare;
- information technology;
- entrepreneurship;
- government service.

Professional experience must demonstrate measurable competencies.

---

## Military Education and Training

Military education may be evaluated using:

- official transcripts;
- military occupations;
- documented training;
- leadership experience;
- technical certifications.

---

## Industry Certifications

Examples include:

- cloud certifications;
- cybersecurity certifications;
- project management;
- accounting;
- healthcare;
- manufacturing;
- technology.

Industry certifications may satisfy competency requirements where appropriate.

---

## Independent Learning

Independent learning may include:

- published research;
- professional publications;
- documented projects;
- open educational resources;
- supervised study;
- verified continuing education.

---

## Portfolio Evidence

Portfolios may demonstrate competency through:

- work products;
- research;
- software;
- presentations;
- business plans;
- publications;
- creative works;
- technical documentation.

---

# PLA Evaluation Methods

The institution may evaluate prior learning using:

- transcript review;
- portfolio assessment;
- competency challenge examinations;
- interviews;
- demonstrations;
- simulations;
- oral examinations;
- practical performance;
- faculty review.

Multiple methods may be combined.

---

# Portfolio Assessment

A portfolio should contain:

- learner narrative;
- competency mapping;
- evidence artifacts;
- supporting documentation;
- employer verification where applicable;
- reflective analysis.

Faculty evaluate portfolios using standardized rubrics.

---

# Challenge Examinations

Challenge examinations allow learners to demonstrate competency without completing standard instructional activities.

Challenge examinations may include:

- written examinations;
- performance assessments;
- oral examinations;
- simulations;
- laboratory demonstrations.

Passing a challenge examination does not guarantee award of academic credit unless institutional requirements are satisfied.

---

# Faculty Review

Qualified faculty evaluate:

- evidence quality;
- competency alignment;
- academic rigor;
- authenticity;
- completeness;
- equivalency.

Faculty retain authority over final competency recognition.

---

# AI-Assisted PLA

Artificial Intelligence may assist by:

- organizing submitted evidence;
- mapping competencies;
- identifying documentation gaps;
- recommending faculty review priorities.

AI recommendations remain advisory.

Human faculty approve all recognition decisions.

---

# Competency Mapping

Every approved PLA decision must map evidence to:

- institutional competencies;
- program learning outcomes;
- course outcomes where applicable;
- educational datasets;
- credential requirements.

Competency mapping supports accreditation documentation.

---

# Recognition Outcomes

PLA may result in:

- competency recognition;
- placement adjustment;
- educational dataset waiver;
- pathway acceleration;
- challenge examination recommendation;
- additional assessment recommendation;
- denial with documented rationale.

Learners receive written explanations for significant decisions.

---

# Tuition Impact

Recognized prior learning reduces unnecessary instructional costs.

Potential tuition adjustments include:

- reduced competency blocks;
- reduced educational dataset requirements;
- accelerated completion timelines;
- lower pathway tuition.

Assessment fees remain separate from tuition unless institutional policy provides otherwise.

---

| PLA Service | Recommended Range |
|-------------|------------------:|
| Portfolio Review | $100–300 |
| Challenge Examination | $100–200 |
| Transcript Evaluation | Included or $50–100 |
| Professional Competency Review | $150–300 |
| Military Learning Review | Included or $100 |

Pricing should remain substantially below equivalent instructional cost.

---

# Documentation Requirements

Every PLA decision should document:

- evidence reviewed;
- evaluator;
- competencies recognized;
- competencies denied;
- rationale;
- date;
- institutional approval.

Documentation becomes part of the learner's academic record.

---

# Appeals

Learners may appeal PLA decisions.

Appeals should include:

- additional evidence;
- clarification;
- procedural concerns;
- documentation corrections.

Appeals are reviewed by qualified academic personnel not solely responsible for the original decision whenever practicable.

---

# Quality Assurance

PLA effectiveness is evaluated using:

- learner success;
- completion rates;
- faculty consistency;
- assessment reliability;
- employer outcomes;
- accreditation feedback.

Regular audits help ensure fairness and consistency.

---

# Relationship to the SydTek Canon

This framework implements:

- Initial Assessment Framework;
- Personalized Learning Pathways;
- Competency Framework;
- SydTek University Academic Architecture.

Prior Learning Assessment supports affordability while maintaining equivalent academic expectations.
