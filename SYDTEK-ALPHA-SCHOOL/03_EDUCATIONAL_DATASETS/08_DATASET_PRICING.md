# Purpose

The Educational Dataset Pricing Standard establishes how SydTek Alpha School prices educational datasets for learners, institutions, employers, governments, nonprofit organizations, and licensing partners.

Educational datasets represent instructional infrastructure rather than traditional textbooks.

Pricing is designed to:

- improve educational affordability;
- support institutional sustainability;
- encourage lifelong learning;
- reduce unnecessary instructional costs;
- maintain transparent pricing;
- support competency-based education;
- enable institutional licensing.

---

# Pricing Philosophy

SydTek prices learning resources rather than instructional time whenever appropriate.

Traditional tuition often charges learners regardless of demonstrated competency.

SydTek instead prices:

- assessments;
- competency pathways;
- educational datasets;
- faculty-intensive services;
- premium AI services;
- institutional licenses.

This model seeks to align learner cost with demonstrated educational need while recognizing that legal, regulatory, and accreditation requirements may still require certain instructional, faculty, or residency elements depending on the program.

---

# Guiding Principles

Educational dataset pricing should be:

- transparent;
- predictable;
- equitable;
- evidence based;
- scalable;
- sustainable;
- accreditation compatible.

Learners should understand estimated educational costs before enrollment.

# K–12 Learning Pathways

Recommended annual pathway pricing:

| Level | Suggested Annual Range |
|----------------------|-----------------------:|
| Kindergarten | $900–1,500 |
| Elementary School | $1,200–2,000 |
| Middle School | $1,800–2,500 |
| High School | $2,200–3,500 |

Annual pathways include:

- educational datasets;
- competency assessments;
- baseline AI tutoring;
- progress monitoring;
- pathway reviews.

Optional premium services are priced separately.

---

# Undergraduate Pathways

Recommended annual learner investment:

**$2,500–5,000**

Estimated bachelor's pathway target:

**$10,000–18,000**

This generally includes:

- competency blocks;
- educational datasets;
- baseline AI tutoring;
- faculty verification;
- pathway management.

Programs with laboratory, clinical, practicum, or other specialized requirements may incur additional published costs.

---

# Graduate Pathways

Typical annual pricing:

**$3,500–6,500**

Graduate pathways include:

- advanced educational datasets;
- faculty mentoring;
- research support;
- competency verification;
- baseline AI tutoring.

---

# Doctoral Pathways

Suggested total pathway range:

**$8,000–18,000**

Pricing reflects:

- dissertation datasets;
- faculty supervision;
- research support;
- dissertation review;

# Institutional Licensing Pricing

Institutional pricing depends upon:

- number of learners;
- deployment scope;
- support requirements;
- update frequency;
- AI integration;
- implementation complexity.

Pricing models may include:

- annual subscription;
- enterprise agreement;
- district license;
- university license;
- consortium agreement.

Institutional pricing is negotiated separately from individual learner pricing.

# Pricing Transparency

Before enrollment learners should receive:

- assessment pricing;
- pathway estimate;
- dataset requirements;
- optional services;
- refund policy;
- reassessment pricing;
- estimated maximum educational investment.

Transparent pricing strengthens learner trust and institutional accountability.

---

# Pricing Review

Pricing should be reviewed annually using evidence such as:

- enrollment;
- affordability;
- completion rates;
- learner satisfaction;
- faculty workload;
- institutional sustainability;
- AI operating costs;
- market benchmarking.

Pricing changes should be documented through institutional version-control procedures.
