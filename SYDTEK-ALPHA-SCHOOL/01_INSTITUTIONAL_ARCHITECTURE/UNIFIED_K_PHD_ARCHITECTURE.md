# SydTek Alpha School
# Unified K–PhD Institutional Architecture
**Repository:** SydTek Alpha School  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Document Status:** Approved Institutional Architecture  
**Document Owner:** Governing Board and Chief Academic Officer  
**Applies To:** SydTek Scholars Alpha School and SydTek University
---
# Purpose
This document defines the unified institutional architecture connecting:
- SydTek Scholars Alpha School, serving Kindergarten through Grade 12; and
- SydTek University, serving undergraduate, graduate, professional, and doctoral learners.
The architecture establishes how both divisions operate within one K–PhD ecosystem while maintaining the distinct academic, developmental, legal, governance, faculty, student-support, and accreditation requirements appropriate to each level.
---
# Institutional Identity
SydTek Alpha School is the governing educational ecosystem.
It includes two integrated academic divisions:
```text
SydTek Alpha School
├── SydTek Scholars Alpha School
│   ├── Kindergarten
│   ├── Elementary School
│   ├── Middle School
│   └── High School
│
└── SydTek University
    ├── Undergraduate Education
    ├── Graduate Education
    ├── Professional Education
    └── Doctoral Education

The divisions share institutional infrastructure but do not operate as interchangeable academic units.

Growth Architecture

Expansion must proceed through defined gates.

Gate 1 — Legal Readiness

Required approvals and disclosures are confirmed.

Gate 2 — Academic Readiness

Curriculum, faculty, assessments, and outcomes are approved.

Gate 3 — Operational Readiness

Technology, records, support, and procedures are functional.

Gate 4 — Financial Readiness

Budget, reserves, forecasts, and refund capacity are adequate.

Gate 5 — Quality Readiness

Evidence systems and improvement processes are active.

Gate 6 — Enrollment Authorization

Leadership approves enrollment based on capacity.

No program should bypass these gates.
