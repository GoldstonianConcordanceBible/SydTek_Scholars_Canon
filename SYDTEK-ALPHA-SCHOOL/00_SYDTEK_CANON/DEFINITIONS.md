# SydTek Canon

# Definitions

**Repository:** SydTek Alpha School  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Document Status:** Canonical  
**Applies To:** SydTek Scholars Alpha School and SydTek University

---

# Purpose

This document establishes the official terminology used throughout the SydTek Alpha School repository.

These definitions apply across:

- SydTek Scholars Alpha School
- SydTek University
- Admissions and Assessment
- Curriculum and Instruction
- Educational Datasets
- Artificial Intelligence
- Academic Governance
- Tuition and Pricing
- Faculty and Human Oversight
- Privacy and Security
- Accreditation Readiness
- Institutional Effectiveness
- Financial Sustainability
- Research and Institutional Licensing

Where an operational document uses a defined term, the meaning established in this document governs unless applicable law or a binding external requirement provides otherwise.

---

# Institutional Terms

## SydTek Alpha School

The unified K–PhD institutional framework governed by the SydTek Canon.

SydTek Alpha School consists of:

- SydTek Scholars Alpha School
- SydTek University

The name refers to the complete educational ecosystem unless a document clearly limits its application to one division.

---

## SydTek Scholars Alpha School

The Kindergarten through Grade 12 division of SydTek Alpha School.

It serves learners in:

- Kindergarten
- Elementary School
- Middle School
- High School
- Supplemental Education
- Enrichment
- College Preparation
- Career and Technical Preparation

---

## SydTek University

The postsecondary division of SydTek Alpha School.

It serves:

- Undergraduate learners
- Graduate learners
- Professional learners
- Workforce learners
- Researchers
- Doctoral candidates
- Lifelong learners

Degrees may only be awarded when SydTek possesses the required legal authority.

---

## SydTek Canon

The highest governing authority within the SydTek Alpha School repository.

The Canon establishes:

- Mission
- Scope
- Foundational Principles
- Institutional Architecture
- Governance
- Definitions
- Version Control
- Amendment Procedures

Operational documents derive authority from the SydTek Canon.

---

## Canonical Document

A governing document formally adopted as part of the SydTek Canon.

---

## Operational Document

A policy, procedure, handbook, template, pricing schedule, or implementation document supporting the Canon.

---

# Learner Terms

## Learner

Any individual participating in a SydTek educational activity.

This includes:

- Students
- Scholars
- Researchers
- Professional learners
- Lifelong learners

---

## Student

A learner formally enrolled in SydTek.

---

## Scholar

A learner enrolled within SydTek Scholars Alpha School.

---

## Candidate

A learner formally seeking completion of:

- Graduation
- Certificate
- Degree
- Thesis
- Dissertation

Candidate status does not guarantee completion.

---

## Parent or Guardian

An individual legally authorized to make educational decisions for a minor learner where applicable.

---

# Academic Terms

## Assessment

A structured process used to evaluate:

- Readiness
- Knowledge
- Skill
- Competency
- Prior Learning
- Academic Progress

---

## Initial Assessment

The first institutional evaluation used to determine:

- Placement
- Readiness
- Learning Gaps
- Personalized Pathway

---

## Placement Assessment

Assessment used to determine the appropriate:

- Grade
- Course
- Program
- Pathway

---

## Prior Learning

Knowledge or experience obtained before enrollment.

---

## Prior Learning Assessment

A documented evaluation of prior learning through:

- Portfolio
- Challenge Examination
- Transcript Review
- Demonstration
- Professional Experience

---

## Challenge Examination

An examination allowing a learner to demonstrate mastery without completing the standard instructional sequence.

---

## Competency

A measurable combination of:

- Knowledge
- Skill
- Application
- Judgment
- Performance

---

## Competency-Based Education

An educational model where progression is primarily based upon demonstrated mastery.

---

## Mastery

Verified achievement of an approved competency standard.

---

## Learning Outcome

A statement describing what a learner should know or demonstrate after instruction.

---

## Curriculum

The approved collection of:

- Learning Outcomes
- Instruction
- Assessments
- Activities
- Educational Resources

---

## Instruction

The intentional facilitation of learning through teachers, faculty, educational datasets, laboratories, AI assistance, mentoring, or supervised activities.

---

## Academic Credit

Institutional recognition that approved academic requirements have been completed.

---

## Portfolio

A documented collection of learner work demonstrating competency or achievement.

---

## Capstone

A culminating academic project demonstrating integration of major learning outcomes.

---

## Thesis

A graduate-level scholarly work completed under qualified faculty supervision.

---

## Dissertation

An original doctoral research contribution completed under qualified supervision and institutional approval.

---

# Educational Dataset Terms

## Educational Dataset

A structured, version-controlled instructional repository supporting learning, assessment, competency development, or research.

Educational datasets may contain:

- Lessons
- Learning Outcomes
- Assessments
- Multimedia
- Simulations
- Rubrics
- AI Tutoring Context
- Version History
- Curriculum Metadata

Educational datasets are instructional resources.

They are **not** student education records.

---

## Lesson Dataset

A dataset supporting a single lesson or learning objective.

---

## Module Dataset

A dataset supporting multiple related lessons.

---

## Competency Block

A collection of educational resources organized around one or more competencies.

---

## Course Dataset

A complete instructional repository supporting an approved course.

---

## Grade Dataset

A collection of instructional datasets supporting one grade level.

---

## Laboratory Dataset

An instructional dataset supporting laboratory or simulation-based learning.

---

## Research Dataset

A dataset supporting research instruction or scholarly investigation.

---

# Artificial Intelligence Terms

## Artificial Intelligence

A computational system capable of supporting learning, analysis, reasoning, or content generation.

---

## Generative Artificial Intelligence

AI capable of generating:

- Text
- Images
- Audio
- Video
- Code
- Structured Data

---

## AI Tutor

An AI system supporting instruction through:

- Tutoring
- Explanations
- Practice
- Feedback

AI tutors do not possess academic authority.

---

## Agentic Learning Assistant

An AI system capable of performing multi-step educational tasks under institutional controls.

---

## Human in the Loop

A governance requirement ensuring meaningful human review of AI-supported decisions.

---

## Consequential Decision

A decision materially affecting:

- Admission
- Grades
- Graduation
- Academic Standing
- Credentialing
- Discipline

AI may not independently make consequential academic decisions.

---

Qualified individuals responsible for:

- Teaching
- Assessment
- Curriculum
- Advising
- Research
- Academic Governance

---

## Teacher

A qualified K–12 educator.

---

## Instructor

An individual authorized to deliver instruction.

---

## Academic Advisor

An individual supporting learner progression.

---

## Academic Coach

---

## Subject-Matter Expert

# Credential Terms

## Credential

Formal institutional recognition of learning.

Examples include:

- Badge
- Microcredential
- Certificate
- Diploma
- Degree

---

## Transcript

The official academic record maintained by SydTek.

---

## Learning Record

A broader record of verified learner achievement.

# Tuition Terms

## Assessment-Based Tuition

A pricing model based upon:

- Initial Assessment
- Prior Learning
- Competency Pathway
- Instructional Needs
- Faculty Support

---

## Pathway

A personalized sequence of competencies leading toward a defined academic outcome.

---

## Pathway Cap

The published maximum amount expected for a defined pathway.

---

## Assessment Credit

Assessment fees applied toward tuition under approved institutional policy.

---

## Tuition Credit

A documented reduction in tuition.

---

## Refund

Funds returned according to the institutional refund policy.

---

# Governance Terms

## Governance

The system through which SydTek exercises institutional authority.

---

## Policy

A formally approved institutional requirement.

---

## Procedure

The documented implementation of a policy.

---

## Standard

A required institutional expectation.

---

## Template

A standardized institutional document.

---

## Amendment

An approved change to a canonical document.

---

## Version Control

The documented management of institutional revisions.

# Authorization Terms

## Authorization

Legal permission to operate educational activities.

---

## Degree-Granting Authority

Legal authority to award academic degrees.

---

## Accreditation

Formal recognition granted by an accrediting body after external review.

Accreditation readiness is not accreditation.

---

## Accreditation Readiness

Documented preparation for external accreditation review.

---

# Privacy Terms

## Student Education Record

Records maintained regarding a student's educational participation.

---

## Personally Identifiable Information

Information identifying an individual learner.

---

## De-Identification

Removal or reduction of identifying information.

---

## Consent

Documented permission where legally required.

---

## Accessibility

Design ensuring equitable educational participation.

---

## Academic Integrity

Honest participation in learning, assessment, research, and credentialing.

---

# Financial Terms

## Financial Sustainability

Institutional capacity to maintain continuous educational operations.

---

## Reserve

Funds maintained to support institutional continuity.

---

## Scholarship

Financial assistance reducing tuition.

---

## Institutional License

A contractual right allowing another organization to use SydTek resources under defined conditions.

Institutional licensing does not transfer accreditation or degree authority.

---

# Delphi Research Terms

## Delphi Method

A structured multi-round expert review process designed to develop informed consensus.

---

## Round One

Independent expert responses.

---

## Round Two

Review of synthesized anonymous findings.

---

## Round Three

Final convergence and recommendations.

---

## Consensus

Documented agreement meeting a defined methodological threshold.

---

# Interpretation Rule

Where definitions conflict:

1. Applicable law governs.
2. Regulatory or accreditor definitions govern.
3. The SydTek Canon governs institutional interpretation.
4. Operational policies govern implementation.
