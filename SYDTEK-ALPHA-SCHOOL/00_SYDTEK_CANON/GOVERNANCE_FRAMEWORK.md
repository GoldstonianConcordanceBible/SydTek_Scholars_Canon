# Governance Framework

**Repository:** SydTek Alpha School  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Document Status:** Canonical  
**Applies To:** SydTek Scholars Alpha School and SydTek University

---

# Purpose

This document establishes the governance structure, authority, accountability, decision-making processes, and institutional controls for SydTek Alpha School.

It governs both:

- **SydTek Scholars Alpha School** — Kindergarten through Grade 12
- **SydTek University** — Undergraduate through Doctoral Education

The governance framework is designed to support:

- learner protection;
- academic integrity;
- legal compliance;
- financial sustainability;
- responsible artificial intelligence;
- accreditation readiness;
- institutional continuity;
- transparent decision-making;
- continuous improvement.

---

# Governance Principle

SydTek Alpha School must be governed through documented authority rather than informal influence.

Institutional decisions must be:

- authorized;
- recorded;
- reviewable;
- consistent with the SydTek Canon;
- supported by evidence;
- assigned to accountable individuals or bodies.

No person, department, contractor, partner, donor, or AI system may exercise authority beyond formally approved limits.

---

# Governance Hierarchy

The SydTek governance hierarchy is:

```text
Applicable Law and Binding External Requirements
                    ↓
             Governing Board
                    ↓
              SydTek Canon
                    ↓
      Executive and Academic Leadership
                    ↓
        Faculty and Standing Committees
                    ↓
       Operational Policies and Procedures
                    ↓
       Programs, Departments, and Services
```

Where conflict exists:

1. applicable law governs;
2. binding regulatory or accreditation requirements govern;
3. the Governing Board exercises fiduciary authority;
4. the SydTek Canon governs internal institutional documents;
5. approved policies and procedures govern implementation.

---

# Governing Board

The Governing Board is the highest internal fiduciary authority for SydTek Alpha School.

The Board is responsible for:

- preserving the institutional mission;
- approving the SydTek Canon;
- appointing and evaluating executive leadership;
- approving major academic and financial policies;
- overseeing institutional risk;
- protecting institutional independence;
- monitoring student outcomes;
- approving budgets;
- ensuring adequate financial reserves;
- overseeing accreditation readiness;
- approving major partnerships;
- approving material institutional changes;
- ensuring lawful and ethical operations.

The Board must not delegate its ultimate fiduciary responsibility.

---

# Board Composition

The Governing Board should include individuals with relevant experience in areas such as:

- K–12 education;
- higher education;
- academic governance;
- finance;
- law;
- artificial intelligence;
- cybersecurity;
- student services;
- accreditation;
- workforce development;
- research ethics;
- institutional operations.

The Board should include sufficient independent members to provide objective oversight.

Board composition must avoid excessive concentration of authority among:

- founders;
- family members;
- vendors;
- employees;
- investors;
- affiliated organizations.

---

# Board Independence

A Board member is considered independent when the member can exercise judgment without material personal, financial, employment, family, or contractual conflicts.

The Board should maintain:

- an independence standard;
- annual conflict disclosures;
- recusal procedures;
- documented review of related-party transactions;
- term limits or periodic reappointment review;
- succession planning.

---

# Board Officers

The Board may include:

- Chair;
- Vice Chair;
- Secretary;
- Treasurer;
- other approved officers.

Board officers must have documented roles.

The Board Chair should support effective governance without assuming executive authority unless formally authorized.

---

# Board Meetings

The Board must meet on a documented schedule.

At minimum, the Board should review:

- institutional performance;
- finances;
- enrollment;
- learner outcomes;
- complaints;
- faculty capacity;
- accreditation readiness;
- AI risks;
- privacy and cybersecurity;
- legal and regulatory status;
- strategic priorities.

Meeting records must include:

- date;
- attendees;
- quorum;
- agenda;
- decisions;
- recusals;
- assigned actions;
- approval status.

---

# Board Committees

Recommended Board committees include:

- Academic Quality Committee;
- Finance and Audit Committee;
- Governance and Nominating Committee;
- Risk, Privacy, and Cybersecurity Committee;
- Student Protection Committee;
- Accreditation and Institutional Effectiveness Committee.

Each committee must have:

- a written charter;
- defined authority;
- membership requirements;
- reporting obligations;
- meeting records;
- conflict-of-interest controls.

---

# Executive Leadership

Executive leadership is responsible for implementing Board-approved strategy and policy.

Recommended executive roles include:

- President or Chief Executive Officer;
- Chief Academic Officer or Provost;
- Head of SydTek Scholars Alpha School;
- Head of SydTek University;
- Chief Financial Officer;
- Chief Technology or Information Officer;
- Chief Student Services Officer;
- Chief Compliance or Risk Officer;
- Accreditation Liaison;
- Research Integrity Officer where applicable.

One individual may hold more than one role during early development, but role consolidation must be:

- documented;
- periodically reviewed;
- limited by capacity;
- separated where conflicts could arise.

---

# President or Chief Executive Officer

The President or Chief Executive Officer is responsible for:

- institutional leadership;
- execution of Board-approved strategy;
- legal and regulatory compliance;
- financial stewardship;
- operational performance;
- external representation;
- institutional partnerships;
- risk management;
- leadership appointments;
- Board reporting.

The President may not unilaterally alter the SydTek Canon.

---

Responsibilities include:

- faculty standards;

- program review;

- credential requirements;
- faculty governance;

The Head of SydTek Scholars Alpha School is responsible for:

- parent and guardian communication;
- student records;

# Head of SydTek University

The Head of SydTek University is responsible for:

- undergraduate programs;
- graduate programs;
- professional programs;
- doctoral programs;
- faculty operations;
- research education;
- student progression;
- postsecondary authorization;
- degree licensure;
- university accreditation readiness.

# Academic Governance

Academic governance must include meaningful faculty participation.

Faculty should participate in decisions concerning:

- curriculum;
- academic standards;
- assessment;
- program learning outcomes;
- admissions standards;
- faculty qualifications;
- academic integrity;
- graduation requirements;
- thesis standards;
- dissertation standards;
- program review;
- research standards.

Academic decisions must not be controlled solely by financial, marketing, technical, or executive interests.

---

# Academic Council

SydTek should maintain an Academic Council or equivalent body.

The Academic Council may include:

- Chief Academic Officer;
- K–12 academic leadership;
- university academic leadership;
- faculty representatives;
- assessment leadership;
- curriculum leadership;
- student-support representatives;
- accreditation liaison;
- research representatives where applicable.

The Academic Council should review:

- new programs;
- curriculum changes;
- academic policies;
- competency frameworks;
- educational datasets;
- grading standards;
- credential requirements;
- academic appeals trends;
- learning-outcome results.

---

# Faculty Participation

Faculty participation must be documented.

Faculty may contribute through:

- department meetings;
- curriculum committees;
- assessment committees;
- academic integrity panels;
- thesis committees;
- dissertation committees;
- program review teams;
- faculty senate or council structures.

Faculty participation must be meaningful rather than ceremonial.

---

# Student Voice

SydTek should establish appropriate ways for learners to participate in institutional improvement.

Student input may be obtained through:

- surveys;
- student councils;
- listening sessions;
- complaint analysis;
- program reviews;
- exit interviews;
- advisory committees.

Minor learners must participate through age-appropriate methods.

Student input does not replace institutional responsibility but should inform decisions.

---

# Parent and Guardian Voice

For SydTek Scholars Alpha School, parents and guardians should have structured opportunities to provide feedback.

Methods may include:

- advisory councils;
- family surveys;
- conferences;
- complaint procedures;
- policy consultations;
- program reviews.

Parent and guardian participation must not undermine student rights, academic standards, or lawful institutional authority.

# Research Ethics Committee

Where SydTek conducts human-participant or sensitive educational research, it must establish an appropriate research ethics review process.

The committee should review:

- informed consent;
- minors;
- vulnerable populations;
- privacy;
- data minimization;
- risk;
- conflicts of interest;
- publication;
- AI use;
- data retention.

SydTek must determine whether a formal Institutional Review Board or equivalent external review arrangement is required.

# Decision Categories

Institutional decisions should be classified according to significance.

## Routine Operational Decisions

Examples include:

- scheduling;
- routine purchasing;
- standard staffing;
- day-to-day service delivery.

These may be delegated to authorized managers.

## Academic Decisions

Examples include:

- curriculum approval;
- competency standards;
- grading rules;
- graduation requirements;
- thesis and dissertation standards.

These require academic authority and faculty participation.

## Strategic Decisions

Examples include:

- new divisions;
- major partnerships;
- substantial enrollment growth;
- new states or countries;
- major technology platforms.

These require executive review and often Board approval.

## Material Institutional Changes

Examples include:

- ownership change;
- mission change;
- degree expansion;
- major delivery-model change;
- new campus;
- merger;
- closure;
- accreditation change;
- control change.

These require formal Board approval and external notification or approval where applicable.

---

# Delegation of Authority

All delegated authority must be documented.

A delegation should identify:

- authority granted;
- responsible role;
- limits;
- duration;
- reporting obligations;
- escalation requirements;
- revocation process.

Delegated authority may not exceed the authority of the delegating body.

---

# Conflict of Interest

All Board members, executives, faculty leaders, committee members, and relevant employees must disclose actual, potential, or perceived conflicts.

Conflicts may include:

- financial interests;
- family relationships;
- vendor relationships;
- outside employment;
- research interests;
- intellectual-property interests;
- investment interests;
- governance roles in affiliated organizations.

Conflicted individuals must not improperly influence decisions.

Recusal must be documented.

---

# Related-Party Transactions

Related-party transactions require heightened review.

The institution must document:

- the relationship;
- business purpose;
- pricing;
- alternatives considered;
- fairness;
- approval;
- recusals.

Material related-party transactions should receive independent Board review.

---

# Policy Approval

Policies must be approved by the authority assigned to their subject matter.

Examples include:

- Board approval for institutional governance and major financial policies;
- academic approval for curriculum and grading policies;
- executive approval for operational procedures;
- legal or compliance review for regulated matters.

Every policy must identify:

- owner;
- approver;
- effective date;
- version;
- review date;
- related documents.

---

# Canonical Amendments

Changes to the SydTek Canon require:

1. written proposal;
2. rationale;
3. impact analysis;
4. legal and academic review where applicable;
5. consultation with affected governance bodies;
6. Governing Board approval;
7. version update;
8. change-log entry;
9. repository publication;
10. communication to affected stakeholders.

No canonical amendment may be implemented informally.

---

# Emergency Authority

SydTek may establish temporary emergency authority for urgent circumstances involving:

- student safety;
- cybersecurity;
- legal compliance;
- financial continuity;
- facility closure;
- public health;
- technology failure.

Emergency decisions must be:

- limited in duration;
- documented;
- proportionate;
- reviewed afterward;
- reported to the appropriate authority.

Emergency authority may not be used to permanently bypass governance.

---

# Institutional Risk Management

SydTek must maintain a documented risk-management process.

Risk categories should include:

- academic quality;
- student safety;
- finance;
- legal compliance;
- accreditation;
- privacy;
- cybersecurity;
- AI;
- reputation;
- faculty capacity;
- enrollment;
- research;
- vendor dependence;
- institutional continuity.

Each material risk should have:

- owner;
- likelihood;
- impact;
- controls;
- mitigation plan;
- review date;
- escalation threshold.

---

# Whistleblower and Non-Retaliation Protection

SydTek should maintain a process for reporting suspected:

- fraud;
- misconduct;
- academic violations;
- safety concerns;
- privacy violations;
- financial irregularities;
- conflicts of interest;
- regulatory noncompliance.

Good-faith reporting must be protected from retaliation.

---

# Records of Governance

Governance records must be retained according to an approved schedule.

Records may include:

- bylaws;
- charters;
- minutes;
- resolutions;
- policies;
- conflict disclosures;
- committee reports;
- risk registers;
- accreditation records;
- financial approvals;
- canonical amendments.

Sensitive governance records must be protected while remaining available to authorized reviewers.

---

# Governance Transparency

SydTek should publicly disclose appropriate information concerning:

- mission;
- leadership;
- governance;
- authorization;
- accreditation status;
- tuition;
- complaints;
- academic programs;
- consumer protections.

Confidential information should remain protected.

Transparency does not require publication of sensitive student, personnel, security, or privileged information.

---

# Annual Governance Review

At least annually, the Governing Board should review:

- Board composition;
- independence;
- committee performance;
- executive performance;
- institutional risk;
- mission alignment;
- financial condition;
- academic quality;
- student outcomes;
- accreditation readiness;
- policy currency;
- succession planning.

The Board should document improvement actions.

---

# Governance Readiness Standard

Governance reaches a 10/10 internal readiness level only when SydTek has:

- legally valid organizational documents;
- an active Governing Board;
- independent oversight;
- approved bylaws;
- conflict controls;
- documented delegations;
- functioning academic governance;
- functioning committees;
- meeting minutes;
- risk oversight;
- policy controls;
- succession plans;
- annual review evidence.

A written framework alone does not establish readiness.
