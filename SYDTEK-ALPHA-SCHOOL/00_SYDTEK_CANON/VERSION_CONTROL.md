# SydTek Canon

# Version Control and Document Governance

**Repository:** SydTek Alpha School  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Document Status:** Canonical  
**Applies To:** SydTek Scholars Alpha School and SydTek University

---

# Purpose

This document establishes the version-control, amendment, approval, publication, archival, and document-governance requirements for the SydTek Alpha School repository.

It applies to:

- the SydTek Canon;
- institutional policies;
- academic standards;
- pricing schedules;
- handbooks;
- educational datasets;
- assessment systems;
- AI governance documents;
- accreditation evidence;
- templates;
- public disclosures;
- institutional records.

The purpose of version control is to ensure that every official SydTek document is:

- identifiable;
- authoritative;
- current;
- reviewable;
- traceable;
- consistently approved;
- protected from unauthorized alteration.

---

# Governing Principle

No institutional document should exist without a known owner, version, status, approval authority, and review date.

Material changes must be documented before implementation.

The repository must preserve enough history to determine:

- what changed;
- when it changed;
- why it changed;
- who proposed it;
- who reviewed it;
- who approved it;
- what evidence supported it;
- which learners, employees, or programs were affected.

---

# Document Hierarchy

SydTek documents are organized into five levels.

## Level 1 — Canonical Documents

Canonical documents establish the institution’s highest internal principles and authority.

Examples include:

- `README.md`;
- `CANONICAL_INDEX.md`;
- `MISSION_AND_SCOPE.md`;
- `FOUNDATIONAL_PRINCIPLES.md`;
- `K_PHD_MODEL.md`;
- `DEFINITIONS.md`;
- `GOVERNANCE_FRAMEWORK.md`;
- `VERSION_CONTROL.md`.

Canonical documents require the highest level of review and approval.

---

## Level 2 — Institutional Policies

Institutional policies establish mandatory requirements governing major areas such as:

- admissions;
- tuition;
- refunds;
- privacy;
- academic integrity;
- faculty qualifications;
- complaints;
- accessibility;
- artificial intelligence;
- cybersecurity;
- research ethics.

Policies must be consistent with the SydTek Canon.

---

## Level 3 — Standards

Standards define required levels of quality or performance.

Examples include:

- educational dataset standards;
- assessment standards;
- faculty workload standards;
- accessibility standards;
- AI audit standards;
- accreditation evidence standards.

---

## Level 4 — Procedures

Procedures describe how policies and standards are implemented.

Examples include:

- assessment administration;
- grade appeals;
- dataset review;
- student record requests;
- incident response;
- faculty credential verification;
- refund processing.

---

## Level 5 — Templates and Forms

Templates and forms support consistent implementation.

Examples include:

- learning plans;
- assessment reports;
- approval forms;
- committee minutes;
- evidence logs;
- grievance forms;
- pricing disclosures.

Templates may not override policies or standards.

---

# Required Document Metadata

Every controlled document must include:

- institution or repository name;
- document title;
- version number;
- document status;
- document owner;
- approval authority;
- effective date;
- review date;
- applicable division;
- superseded version where applicable.

Recommended header format:

```text
Repository: SydTek Alpha School
Document: [Document Title]
Version: 1.0.0
Status: Draft | Under Review | Approved | Canonical | Retired
Owner: [Role or Committee]
Approved By: [Authority]
Effective Date: YYYY-MM-DD
Next Review Date: YYYY-MM-DD
Applies To: [Division or Institution]
```

# Version Numbering

SydTek uses semantic versioning for institutional documents.

Format:

```text
MAJOR.MINOR.PATCH
```

Example:

```text
1.2.3
```

## Major Version

Increase the major version when a change materially affects:

- mission;
- governance;
- academic standards;
- tuition structure;
- credential requirements;
- legal rights;
- institutional scope;
- accreditation commitments;
- AI authority;
- student protections.

Example:

```text
1.0.0 → 2.0.0
```

---

## Minor Version

Increase the minor version when a change adds or substantially revises requirements without changing the document’s core authority.

Examples include:

- adding a new procedure;
- expanding a standard;
- adding a new pathway;
- revising documentation requirements;
- adding a new committee responsibility.

Example:

```text
1.0.0 → 1.1.0
```

---

## Patch Version

Increase the patch version for non-material corrections such as:

- spelling;
- formatting;
- broken links;
- clarification that does not alter meaning;
- metadata corrections;
- reference updates.

Example:

```text
1.0.0 → 1.0.1
```

# Document Status

Each controlled document must use one of the following statuses.

## Draft

The document is being developed and has not been approved.

Draft documents must not be treated as institutional policy.

---

## Under Review

The document has entered a formal review process.

It may not be implemented unless temporary approval is documented.

---

## Approved

The document has received the required approval and may be implemented.

---

## Canonical

The document is part of the SydTek Canon and holds the highest internal documentary authority.

---

## Superseded

The document has been replaced by a newer approved version.

Superseded documents must remain archived.

---

## Retired

The document is no longer active and has no approved successor.

Retirement must be documented.

---

## Emergency Temporary

The document or change is temporarily approved to address an urgent circumstance.

Emergency status must include:

- reason;
- approving authority;
- effective period;
- expiration date;
- review requirement.

---

# Document Ownership

Every controlled document must have an assigned owner.

The document owner is responsible for:

- maintaining accuracy;
- initiating review;
- monitoring legal and regulatory changes;
- coordinating amendments;
- maintaining evidence;
- communicating updates;
- confirming implementation.

The document owner should be a role or committee rather than only an individual name.

Examples include:

- Chief Academic Officer;
- AI Governance Committee;
- Finance and Audit Committee;
- Director of Student Services;
- Privacy Officer;
- Accreditation Steering Committee.

---

# Approval Authority

Approval authority depends on document type.

## Governing Board Approval

Required for:

- Canon documents;
- mission changes;
- governance changes;
- major tuition policy;
- major financial policy;
- institutional closure;
- material organizational changes;
- major accreditation commitments.

## Academic Approval

Required for:

- curriculum;
- program outcomes;
- grading;
- assessment;
- credit;
- graduation requirements;
- thesis and dissertation standards;
- academic integrity.

## Executive Approval

Required for:

- routine operations;
- administrative procedures;
- staffing procedures;
- procurement;
- implementation guides.

## Legal or Compliance Review

Required where documents affect:

- student rights;
- privacy;
- contracts;
- accreditation claims;
- authorization;
- refunds;
- consumer disclosures;
- employment;
- research involving humans;
- accessibility.

A document may require multiple approvals.

# Change Proposal Requirements

A proposed material change must include:

1. document name;
2. current version;
3. proposed version;
4. proposed change;
5. reason for change;
6. affected sections;
7. affected learners or staff;
8. academic impact;
9. financial impact;
10. legal or regulatory impact;
11. technology impact;
12. accreditation impact;
13. implementation date;
14. communication plan;
15. required training;
16. responsible owner.

---

# Change Classification

Changes must be classified before approval.

## Administrative Change

A low-risk operational or formatting adjustment.

## Academic Change

A change affecting:

- curriculum;
- assessment;
- grading;
- credit;
- progression;
- graduation;
- credentials.

## Financial Change

A change affecting:

- tuition;
- fees;
- refunds;
- scholarships;
- institutional licensing;
- reserves;
- student financial obligations.

## Regulatory Change

A change required by:

- law;
- regulation;
- authorization;
- accreditation;
- professional standards.

## Technology Change

A change affecting:

- AI systems;
- educational platforms;
- data systems;
- cybersecurity;
- identity verification;
- system integrations.

## Canonical Change

A change affecting the SydTek Canon.

---

# Canonical Amendment Process

Canonical amendments require:

1. written amendment proposal;
2. rationale;
3. impact analysis;
4. review by the document owner;
5. academic review where applicable;
6. legal or compliance review;
7. institutional consultation;
8. Governing Board approval;
9. version increment;
10. change-log entry;
11. publication;
12. stakeholder notification;
13. implementation monitoring.

No Canon document may be altered directly without this process.

---

# Operational Amendment Process

Operational documents may use a shorter process when risk is lower.

At minimum:

1. change proposal;
2. owner review;
3. required academic, legal, financial, or technical review;
4. approval;
5. version increment;
6. publication;
7. communication;
8. archival of prior version.

---

# Emergency Change Process

Emergency changes may be used when delay could create serious risk involving:

- student safety;
- legal compliance;
- cybersecurity;
- privacy;
- financial continuity;
- technology failure;
- institutional closure;
- public health.

Emergency changes must:

- be approved by authorized leadership;
- be documented immediately;
- include an expiration date;
- receive formal review as soon as practicable;
- be converted to a permanent approved version or withdrawn.

Emergency changes must not become permanent by inaction.

---

# Change Log

The repository must maintain a central `CHANGELOG.md`.

Each material change should include:

- date;
- document;
- old version;
- new version;
- summary;
- change category;
- approving authority;
- effective date.

Recommended format:

```markdown
## 2026-07-12

### 00_SYDTEK_CANON/VERSION_CONTROL.md

- Previous Version: 1.0.0
- New Version: 1.1.0
- Change Type: Minor
- Summary: Added emergency document-control procedures.
- Approved By: Governing Board
- Effective Date: 2026-08-01
```

# Repository Branching

Recommended Git branch structure:

```text
main
develop
policy-review
accreditation-review
dataset-review
```

## Main Branch

Contains only approved and active documents.

## Develop Branch

Contains documents under active development.

## Review Branches

May be used for:

- policy review;
- accreditation crosswalks;
- curriculum review;
- pricing updates;
- educational dataset revisions.

Drafts must not be merged into `main` until approved.

---

# Pull Requests

Material changes should be submitted through a pull request.

A pull request should include:

- document changed;
- version change;
- summary;
- rationale;
- reviewers;
- approval evidence;
- implementation implications;
- related issue or decision record.

Recommended pull-request checklist:

```markdown
- [ ] Document owner reviewed
- [ ] Version updated
- [ ] Change log updated
- [ ] Academic review completed
- [ ] Legal or compliance review completed
- [ ] Financial review completed
- [ ] Accessibility reviewed
- [ ] Accreditation impact reviewed
- [ ] Required approval documented
- [ ] Prior version archived
```

---

# Protected Branches

The `main` branch should be protected.

Recommended protections include:

- no direct commits;
- required pull requests;
- required reviewers;
- required status checks;
- approval before merge;
- restricted administrator bypass;
- signed commits where feasible;
- audit logging.

---

# Issue Tracking

Proposed changes should be tracked through issues.

Issue categories may include:

- policy revision;
- accreditation gap;
- curriculum update;
- pricing change;
- AI risk;
- privacy issue;
- student protection;
- accessibility;
- legal change;
- dataset maintenance.

Each issue should identify:

- owner;
- priority;
- due date;
- affected documents;
- required evidence;
- status.

---

# Decision Records

Major institutional decisions should have a decision record.

Recommended format:

```text
DECISION-YYYY-NNN
```

Each decision record should include:

- issue;
- context;
- options considered;
- evidence;
- decision;
- approving authority;
- date;
- dissent where relevant;
- implementation owner;
- review date.

Decision records should be stored in:

```text
00_SYDTEK_CANON/decisions/
```

# Educational Dataset Version Control

Each educational dataset must have its own version.

Dataset metadata should include:

- dataset title;
- dataset identifier;
- grade, course, or program;
- version;
- academic owner;
- approver;
- effective date;
- next review date;
- prerequisite;
- learning outcomes;
- accessibility status;
- licensing status;
- AI context status;
- assessment status;
- change summary.

Dataset changes affecting learning outcomes or assessment validity require academic approval.

---

# Assessment Version Control

Assessments require heightened controls.

The institution must track:

- assessment version;
- item changes;
- scoring changes;
- mastery threshold changes;
- validation evidence;
- approved administration period;
- security classification;
- access permissions.

Active assessments must not be publicly exposed in the repository.

Public repositories should contain only:

- templates;
- standards;
- sample items;
- redacted evidence.

---

# AI System Version Control

Every approved AI system must have a controlled record identifying:

- system name;
- vendor;
- model version;
- configuration;
- approved uses;
- prohibited uses;
- data access;
- risk classification;
- evaluation results;
- human-review requirements;
- approval date;
- review date.

Material model or vendor changes require reassessment.

---

# Pricing Version Control

Every pricing schedule must include:

- effective date;
- applicable learner group;
- mandatory charges;
- optional charges;
- pathway cap;
- refund treatment;
- grandfathering rules;
- approval authority.

Pricing changes must not be applied retroactively unless:

- legally required;
- clearly authorized by an existing agreement;
- favorable to the learner.

---

# Student-Facing Policy Changes

Changes affecting learners must be communicated before they become effective.

Communication should include:

- what changed;
- why it changed;
- effective date;
- affected learners;
- action required;
- appeal or contact process;
- whether existing learners are grandfathered.

Material changes should be published through:

- website;
- handbook;
- learner portal;
- email or other direct notice.

---

# Grandfathering

SydTek must determine whether existing learners remain under prior requirements.

Grandfathering decisions should consider:

- fairness;
- academic integrity;
- legal requirements;
- accreditation expectations;
- financial effect;
- student progress;
- operational feasibility.

Grandfathering terms must be documented.

---

# Effective Dates

Every approved document must have an effective date.

The effective date may differ from the approval date to allow time for:

- training;
- technical implementation;
- learner communication;
- contract updates;
- operational preparation.

---

# Review Cycles

Recommended review cycles:

| Document Type | Review Frequency |
|---|---:|
| SydTek Canon | Every 2 years or upon material change |
| Institutional policies | Annually |
| Academic standards | Annually |
| Pricing | At least annually |
| Educational datasets | Every 6–12 months |
| AI systems | At least every 6 months |
| Privacy and cybersecurity | At least annually and after incidents |
| Accreditation evidence | Ongoing |
| Emergency policies | Before expiration |

More frequent review may be required by law, risk, or accreditor standards.

---

# Archival Requirements

Superseded documents must be retained.

The archive should preserve:

- prior version;
- approval evidence;
- effective period;
- reason for replacement;
- related change log;
- related decision record.

Recommended archive structure:

```text
archive/
├── canonical/
├── policies/
├── pricing/
├── curriculum/
├── datasets/
├── ai/
└── accreditation/
```

Archived documents must not appear to be current.

---

# Retention

Document retention periods should be based on:

- law;
- accreditation;
- contractual obligations;
- student-record requirements;
- financial requirements;
- litigation risk;
- institutional history.

Permanent retention is recommended for:

- Canon documents;
- Board minutes;
- accreditation decisions;
- degree authority;
- institutional licenses;
- major academic standards;
- teach-out records;
- closure records.

---

# Public and Restricted Documents

Documents should be classified as:

## Public

May be published openly.

Examples:

- mission;
- public policies;
- pricing;
- accreditation status;
- program descriptions;
- governance summaries.

## Internal

Available only to authorized SydTek personnel.

Examples:

- procedures;
- internal reports;
- operational plans.

## Restricted

Requires limited access.

Examples:

- assessment answer keys;
- security controls;
- confidential personnel records;
- student information;
- legal advice;
- incident reports.

## Highly Restricted

Requires the highest level of protection.

Examples:

- authentication credentials;
- encryption keys;
- detailed security vulnerabilities;
- sensitive research data;
- protected health or disability information.

---

# Repository Security

Repository access must follow least-privilege principles.

Controls should include:

- multi-factor authentication;
- role-based access;
- branch protections;
- access reviews;
- audit logs;
- secure secrets management;
- backup;
- incident response;
- removal of access when roles change.

Passwords, API keys, student data, and protected assessment materials must not be stored in public Git history.

---

# Backup and Recovery

The repository must have:

- regular backups;
- geographically separate recovery copies where feasible;
- restoration testing;
- access controls;
- documented recovery procedures.

Backups should preserve:

- files;
- version history;
- approvals;
- issue records;
- pull requests;
- decision records;
- change logs.

---

# Document Consistency Review

Before approval, material documents should be reviewed for consistency with:

- mission and scope;
- foundational principles;
- definitions;
- K–PhD model;
- governance framework;
- pricing policy;
- privacy requirements;
- AI governance;
- accreditation status.

Conflicts must be resolved before publication.

---

# External References

External laws, standards, and guidance may change.

Documents relying on external references must include:

- source title;
- issuing body;
- access date;
- version or publication date where available.

External references should be reviewed during each document cycle.

---

# Audit Readiness

The document-control system must allow an authorized reviewer to determine:

- which document was active on a given date;
- who approved it;
- what version applied to a learner;
- whether the required review occurred;
- whether stakeholders were notified;
- whether implementation was completed.

---

# Noncompliance

Unauthorized document changes may result in:

- rollback;
- incident review;
- retraining;
- disciplinary action;
- access restriction;
- legal review;
- regulatory notification where required.

No institutional officer is exempt from document-control requirements.

---

# Version-Control Readiness Standard

Version-control governance reaches a 10/10 internal readiness level only when SydTek has:

- complete document metadata;
- protected branches;
- formal approval workflows;
- active change log;
- archived prior versions;
- decision records;
- issue tracking;
- access controls;
- backup and recovery;
- periodic audits;
- evidence that staff follow the process.

A repository structure alone does not establish readiness.

---

This document is part of the SydTek Canon.

All SydTek documents, educational datasets, assessments, pricing schedules, AI systems, and public disclosures must follow this version-control framework.

Unauthorized changes have no institutional authority.
