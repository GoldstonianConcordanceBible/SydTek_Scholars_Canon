# SydTek Canon

# Foundational Principles

**Repository:** SydTek Alpha School  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Document Status:** Canonical  
**Applies To:** SydTek Scholars Alpha School and SydTek University

---

# Purpose

This document establishes the foundational principles governing SydTek Alpha School as a unified K–PhD educational ecosystem.

These principles apply to:

- institutional governance;
- curriculum;
- admissions;
- assessment;
- educational datasets;
- artificial intelligence;
- faculty;
- pricing;
- student services;
- accreditation readiness;
- financial sustainability;
- partnerships;
- research;
- institutional licensing.

No operational policy may contradict these principles without an approved amendment to the SydTek Canon.

---

# Principle 1 — The Learner Comes First

SydTek exists to serve learners.

Institutional decisions must prioritize:

- academic quality;
- learner safety;
- accessibility;
- affordability;
- privacy;
- transparency;
- fair treatment;
- meaningful educational outcomes;
- long-term learner development.

Revenue, technology, growth, prestige, and institutional convenience must not take priority over student protection or academic integrity.

---

# Principle 2 — Assessment Before Instruction

Every learner should begin with an appropriate assessment before being assigned an educational pathway.

Assessment should identify:

- current academic readiness;
- demonstrated competencies;
- prior learning;
- learning gaps;
- support needs;
- accessibility requirements;
- recommended placement;
- appropriate academic pathway.

Learners should not be required to repeat content they can already demonstrate they have mastered.

Assessment must be appropriate to the learner’s age, program level, and educational purpose.

---

# Principle 3 — Competency Before Seat Time

Academic progress should be based primarily on demonstrated mastery.

Time may remain relevant for:

- attendance requirements;
- supervised practice;
- laboratories;
- internships;
- clinical or field experience;
- age-appropriate K–12 obligations;
- regulatory requirements.

However, seat time alone does not prove learning.

Credit, promotion, and completion must require evidence of competency.

---

# Principle 4 — Evidence Before Credential

No learner earns a credential solely by:

- paying tuition;
- purchasing a dataset;
- opening content;
- attending without participation;
- completing automated activities without verification.

Credentials must be supported by documented evidence of learning.

Evidence may include:

- examinations;
- authenticated projects;
- portfolios;
- oral defenses;
- laboratory work;
- supervised demonstrations;
- field experiences;
- capstones;
- theses;
- dissertations.

---

# Principle 5 — Personalization Within Academic Standards

SydTek may personalize:

- pace;
- sequence;
- instructional format;
- support intensity;
- dataset assignment;
- learning activities;
- assessment opportunities.

Personalization must not weaken:

- program outcomes;
- academic rigor;
- faculty oversight;
- graduation requirements;
- credential integrity;
- legal obligations.

Different pathways may lead to the same standard, but the standard itself must remain clear and defensible.

---

# Principle 6 — Artificial Intelligence Must Remain Accountable to Humans

Artificial intelligence may support teaching, learning, research, administration, and institutional analysis.

AI must remain subject to:

- named human ownership;
- documented purpose;
- quality controls;
- privacy safeguards;
- security safeguards;
- age-appropriate protections;
- bias testing;
- accessibility review;
- monitoring;
- appeal and correction procedures.

AI may recommend, assist, organize, explain, and analyze.

AI may not independently exercise final authority over consequential academic or disciplinary decisions.

---

# Principle 7 — Human Educators Retain Academic Authority

Qualified human educators and institutional officers remain responsible for:

- curriculum approval;
- admissions decisions;
- final grading;
- academic standing;
- competency verification;
- graduation approval;
- accommodation decisions;
- misconduct findings;
- appeals;
- thesis approval;
- dissertation approval;
- credential issuance.

Human oversight must be meaningful, documented, and proportionate to the importance of the decision.

---

# Principle 8 — Educational Datasets Are Instructional Assets

A SydTek educational dataset is a structured, version-controlled instructional resource.

It may contain:

- learning outcomes;
- instructional content;
- assessments;
- rubrics;
- multimedia;
- simulations;
- projects;
- AI context;
- competency mappings;
- revision history;
- licensing information;
- accessibility information.

Educational datasets must not be confused with student education records.

The institution must maintain technical, legal, and operational separation between instructional assets and identifiable learner data.

---

# Principle 9 — Student Data Is Not a Product

SydTek must not sell identifiable student education records.

Student data may be used only for legitimate, disclosed, and authorized purposes such as:

- instruction;
- assessment;
- advising;
- institutional operations;
- legal compliance;
- approved research;
- institutional improvement.

Data use must follow:

- applicable law;
- documented consent where required;
- minimum-necessary principles;
- access controls;
- retention schedules;
- deletion requirements;
- security protections.

---

# Principle 10 — Pricing Must Be Transparent

Every learner must receive clear information about:

- initial assessment costs;
- tuition;
- mandatory fees;
- optional services;
- pathway caps;
- refunds;
- withdrawal consequences;
- credential fees;
- external costs;
- possible future charges.

SydTek must not conceal mandatory costs or use misleading price comparisons.

Pricing should reflect the learner’s pathway and the institution’s real cost of delivering quality education.

---

# Principle 11 — Prior Learning Should Reduce Unnecessary Cost

Verified prior learning may reduce:

- required coursework;
- required datasets;
- time to completion;
- total tuition;
- duplicated assessment.

Prior learning must be evaluated through a documented and academically defensible process.

The institution must not award credit merely because a learner claims experience.

---

# Principle 12 — Affordability Must Not Undermine Quality

SydTek should pursue affordability through:

- AI-supported efficiency;
- reusable educational datasets;
- competency-based progression;
- prior-learning recognition;
- shared infrastructure;
- institutional licensing;
- scalable services;
- transparent pathway caps.

Affordability must not be achieved by eliminating necessary:

- faculty oversight;
- student support;
- assessment security;
- privacy safeguards;
- accessibility;
- records administration;
- quality assurance;
- financial reserves.

---

# Principle 13 — Accessibility Is a Design Requirement

Accessibility must be considered during:

- curriculum design;
- dataset creation;
- platform development;
- assessment design;
- communications;
- student support;
- facilities planning;
- procurement.

SydTek should not treat accessibility only as a reactive accommodation process.

Learners must have a clear method for requesting accommodations and appealing related decisions.

---

# Principle 14 — Academic Integrity Applies to Humans and Machines

Academic integrity requirements apply to:

- students;
- faculty;
- staff;
- contractors;
- AI systems;
- institutional partners.

SydTek must address:

- plagiarism;
- unauthorized assistance;
- identity fraud;
- fabricated evidence;
- manipulated research;
- misuse of generative AI;
- assessment compromise;
- credential fraud.

Rules must be clear, proportionate, educationally appropriate, and subject to appeal.

---

# Principle 15 — Governance Must Be Documented

Institutional authority must be defined through:

- bylaws;
- approved policies;
- committee charters;
- delegated authority;
- meeting minutes;
- conflict-of-interest controls;
- documented decisions;
- version-controlled records.

Informal influence must not replace accountable governance.

---

# Principle 16 — Academic Governance Requires Faculty Participation

Faculty must have an appropriate role in:

- curriculum;
- academic standards;
- assessment;
- program review;
- faculty qualifications;
- academic integrity;
- graduation requirements;
- research standards.

Business, technical, or financial leadership must not unilaterally override academic standards.

---

# Principle 17 — Growth Must Follow Capacity

SydTek must not enroll more learners than it can responsibly support.

Growth decisions must consider:

- faculty capacity;
- advising capacity;
- platform performance;
- student-support staffing;
- assessment capacity;
- doctoral supervision;
- privacy and security;
- financial reserves;
- regulatory authorization.

Expansion must follow evidence, not aspiration alone.

---

# Principle 18 — Financial Sustainability Is a Student-Protection Requirement

Financial sustainability is not only an institutional concern.

It protects learners from:

- disrupted instruction;
- abandoned programs;
- unpaid faculty;
- lost records;
- unavailable support;
- failed technology;
- incomplete credentials.

SydTek must maintain appropriate:

- forecasts;
- reserves;
- risk controls;
- refund capacity;
- teach-out planning;
- board oversight.

---

# Principle 19 — Accreditation Readiness Is Continuous

Accreditation readiness is not a one-time document exercise.

It requires ongoing evidence of:

- governance;
- qualified faculty;
- learning outcomes;
- student achievement;
- financial stability;
- institutional effectiveness;
- transparency;
- continuous improvement.

Internal readiness must never be represented as external approval.

---

# Principle 20 — Authorization and Accreditation Must Be Represented Accurately

SydTek must accurately disclose its status regarding:

- K–12 registration;
- state authorization;
- degree-granting authority;
- institutional accreditation;
- programmatic accreditation;
- federal financial aid;
- interstate authorization;
- professional licensure alignment.

The institution must not imply that an application, review, partnership, repository, or internal score equals formal approval.

---

# Principle 21 — Research Requires Ethical Governance

Research involving learners, employees, institutional data, or communities must follow appropriate ethical review.

Research governance should address:

- consent;
- risk;
- privacy;
- conflicts of interest;
- vulnerable populations;
- minors;
- data retention;
- publication;
- authorship;
- AI involvement;
- community impact.

Educational innovation does not eliminate research ethics obligations.

---

# Principle 22 — Institutional Licensing Does Not Transfer Accreditation

SydTek may license:

- curriculum;
- educational datasets;
- technology;
- competency frameworks;
- templates;
- AI infrastructure.

A license does not transfer:

- accreditation;
- legal authorization;
- degree authority;
- credentialing authority;
- faculty authority;
- regulatory approval.

These distinctions must be explicit in all agreements and public statements.

---

# Principle 23 — Continuous Improvement Must Be Evidence-Based

SydTek must regularly evaluate:

- learner achievement;
- completion;
- retention;
- affordability;
- support quality;
- faculty performance;
- AI performance;
- complaints;
- accessibility;
- financial outcomes;
- curriculum relevance.

Improvements must be:

- documented;
- assigned to responsible owners;
- implemented;
- measured;
- reviewed.

---

# Principle 24 — The K–PhD Pathway Must Preserve Developmental Appropriateness

A unified K–PhD ecosystem does not mean identical methods at every level.

SydTek must adapt its practices to:

- learner age;
- developmental stage;
- legal status;
- parental involvement;
- academic maturity;
- research independence;
- safeguarding needs.

Kindergarten instruction, undergraduate education, and doctoral supervision require different structures even when they share a common institutional framework.

---

# Principle 25 — The SydTek Canon Governs the Institution

The SydTek Canon is the highest internal documentary authority for the repository.

All policies, programs, contracts, pricing schedules, technologies, and governance decisions must remain consistent with the Canon.

Where conflict exists:

1. applicable law governs;
2. binding regulatory or accreditor requirements govern;
3. the SydTek Canon governs internal documents;
4. approved operational policies govern implementation.

No individual, department, AI system, or partner may override the Canon without an authorized amendment.

---

# Institutional Test

Every major SydTek decision should be evaluated against the following questions:

1. Does it protect learners?
2. Does it preserve academic rigor?
3. Does it maintain human accountability?
4. Is it transparent?
5. Is it legally and ethically defensible?
6. Is it financially sustainable?
7. Does it support accessibility?
8. Can it be evidenced and audited?
9. Does it align with the unified K–PhD mission?
10. Is it consistent with the SydTek Canon?

A proposal that fails these tests should be revised before approval.

---

This document is part of the SydTek Canon.

Operational documents may explain how these principles are implemented, but they may not weaken or contradict them without formal canonical amendment.
