# Purpose

The Educational Digital Twin Standard establishes how AI-powered digital twins are created, governed, monitored, and retired within SydTek Scholars Alpha School and SydTek University.

Digital twins are AI systems designed to represent institutional knowledge, instructional approaches, or approved educational workflows.

A digital twin is not a legal substitute for a learner, faculty member, administrator, or institutional official.

Digital twins assist educational activities while remaining subject to human oversight and institutional governance.

---

# Educational Philosophy

Every learner benefits from personalized educational support.

Digital twins provide:

- individualized guidance;
- continuous tutoring;
- curriculum navigation;
- competency coaching;
- educational planning;
- institutional knowledge retrieval.

Digital twins extend institutional capacity while preserving human relationships.

---

# Guiding Principles

Educational digital twins should be:

- faculty governed;
- transparent;
- competency aligned;
- privacy preserving;
- continuously monitored;
- version controlled;
- ethically deployed;
- educationally beneficial.

Digital twins should never misrepresent their capabilities or authority.

---

# Categories of Digital Twins

SydTek recognizes several categories of educational digital twins.

## Learner Twin

Supports:

- study planning;
- competency tracking;
- educational recommendations;
- learning reflections;
- pathway navigation.

The learner retains responsibility for educational decisions.

---

## Faculty Twin

Supports faculty by assisting with:

- lesson planning;
- instructional preparation;
- rubric drafting;
- curriculum mapping;
- educational resource retrieval.

Faculty approve all instructional materials before publication.

---

## Advisor Twin

Supports academic advising by providing:

- program guidance;
- pathway recommendations;
- prerequisite information;
- scheduling suggestions;
- institutional resource navigation.

Advisors retain responsibility for official academic guidance.

---

## Research Twin

Supports researchers through:

- literature organization;
- research planning;
- document retrieval;
- coding assistance;
- workflow management.

Researchers remain responsible for scholarly accuracy and ethical conduct.

---

## Institutional Twin

Supports administrative operations including:

- policy retrieval;
- procedural guidance;
- institutional analytics;
- operational recommendations.

Institutional leaders remain responsible for official decisions.

---

# Identity Disclosure

Digital twins should clearly identify themselves as AI systems.

They should disclose:

- they are AI;
- their educational purpose;
- their limitations;
- when human assistance is recommended.

Digital twins should not impersonate individuals without explicit institutional authorization and appropriate safeguards.

---

# Educational Boundaries

Digital twins may:

- explain curriculum;
- recommend datasets;
- summarize concepts;
- suggest learning strategies;
- organize educational resources.

Digital twins should not independently:

- assign grades;
- certify competencies;
- confer credentials;
- approve graduation;
- issue official institutional decisions.

---

# Personalization

Digital twins may personalize educational support using appropriate institutional data and applicable privacy safeguards.

Examples include:

- completed competencies;
- educational goals;
- preferred pacing;
- accessibility preferences;
- approved learning history.

Personalization should support learner success while respecting privacy requirements.

---

# Privacy

Digital twins should minimize collection of personal information.

Protected educational records should only be accessed in accordance with institutional policy and applicable law.

Personally identifiable information should not be incorporated into publicly accessible AI systems.

---

# Human Oversight

Qualified personnel remain responsible for:

- educational decisions;
- competency verification;
- institutional guidance;
- learner welfare;
- policy interpretation.

Digital twins provide recommendations.

Humans remain accountable.

---

# Version Control

Every digital twin should maintain documented versions including:

- AI model;
- retrieval sources;
- instructional scope;
- approved datasets;
- review dates;
- known limitations.

Major revisions should follow institutional governance procedures.

---

# Quality Assurance

Digital twins should be evaluated using:

- instructional accuracy;
- learner satisfaction;
- faculty feedback;
- retrieval quality;
- hallucination frequency;
- accessibility;
- response consistency.

Quality improvements should be documented through version control.

---

# Security

Institutional safeguards should include:

- authentication where appropriate;
- authorization controls;
- logging;
- audit capability;
- secure data storage;
- monitoring for misuse.

Security requirements increase with institutional risk.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Digital twins improve through:

- updated educational datasets;
- revised AI Context Datasets;
- faculty recommendations;
- learner feedback;
- instructional analytics;
- governance reviews.

Continuous improvement should remain evidence based.

---

# Relationship to Educational Datasets

Digital twins retrieve information from:

- Educational Datasets;
- AI Context Datasets;
- Competency Frameworks;
- Institutional Policies;
- Learning Outcome Repositories.

Educational datasets remain the authoritative institutional knowledge source.

---

# Relationship to the SydTek Canon

The Educational Digital Twin Standard implements:

- AI Governance Framework;
- Human Oversight Standard;
- AI Tutoring Standard;
- Educational Dataset Standards;
- Competency Framework.

Digital twins extend institutional knowledge while preserving human authority, academic integrity, and institutional accountability.
