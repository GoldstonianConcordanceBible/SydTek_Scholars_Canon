# Purpose

The Artificial Intelligence Governance Framework establishes the institutional governance model for the design, acquisition, deployment, operation, monitoring, and retirement of Artificial Intelligence systems throughout SydTek Alpha School and SydTek University.

The framework ensures that AI advances learning while preserving:

- academic integrity;
- learner success;
- faculty authority;
- institutional accountability;
- legal compliance;
- privacy;
- accessibility;
- public trust.

Artificial Intelligence is an institutional capability—not an institutional authority.

---

# Governance Philosophy

SydTek adopts a **Human-Governed, AI-Augmented** institutional model.

Under this model:

- people establish institutional policy;
- faculty govern curriculum;
- administrators govern operations;
- governing boards govern strategy;
- AI augments approved institutional work.

Authority flows from institutional governance rather than technological capability.

---

# Guiding Principles

Every AI system operating within SydTek should demonstrate:

1. Human oversight
2. Academic integrity
3. Transparency
4. Accountability
5. Privacy protection
6. Accessibility
7. Security
8. Fairness
9. Continuous improvement
10. Institutional alignment

These principles apply across K–12, undergraduate, graduate, doctoral, and administrative environments.

---

# Governance Objectives

The framework supports the following objectives:

- improve learner outcomes;
- personalize instruction;
- reduce administrative burden;
- strengthen faculty effectiveness;
- expand educational access;
- improve institutional analytics;
- support accreditation;
- improve affordability;
- promote responsible innovation;
- maintain public confidence.

Every AI deployment should support at least one institutional objective.

# Governance Structure

Institutional AI governance follows a layered model.

```text
Board of Trustees

        │

        ▼

President

        │

        ▼

Chief Academic Officer

        │

        ▼

AI Governance Committee

        │

 ├───────────────┬───────────────┬───────────────┐

 ▼               ▼               ▼               ▼

Academic     Technology     Privacy      Research

Review        Review         Review       Review

        │

        ▼

Operational AI Teams
```

Operational responsibility may be delegated.

Institutional accountability remains with institutional leadership.

# Roles and Responsibilities

## Governing Board

Responsible for:

- institutional AI strategy;
- major policy approval;
- fiduciary oversight;
- institutional risk.

---

## President

Responsible for:

- institutional implementation;
- executive accountability;
- resource allocation;
- strategic priorities.

---

## Chief Academic Officer

Responsible for:

- instructional AI;
- faculty governance;
- curriculum integration;
- academic quality.

---

## AI Governance Committee

Responsible for:

- policy development;
- risk review;
- AI approval;
- institutional standards;
- periodic review.

The committee should include representatives from academic, technical, legal, accessibility, privacy, and research functions.

---

## Faculty

Faculty remain responsible for:

- instructional decisions;
- grading;
- competency verification;
- curriculum development;
- learner mentoring.

AI may assist faculty.

AI does not replace faculty authority.

---

## Information Technology

Technology teams manage:

- infrastructure;
- deployment;
- integration;
- monitoring;
- cybersecurity;
- system availability.

Every significant AI implementation follows the same workflow.

```text
Proposal

↓

Educational Review

↓

Technical Review

↓

Privacy Review

↓

Accessibility Review

↓

Risk Assessment

↓

Pilot

↓

Institutional Approval

↓

Production Deployment

↓

Continuous Monitoring
```

High-impact AI systems require broader institutional review.

---

# AI Decision Authority

AI systems may:

- recommend;
- summarize;
- classify;
- organize;
- prioritize;
- personalize.

AI systems should not independently:

- admit learners;
- award grades;
- grant degrees;
- confer competencies;
- terminate employment;
- determine disciplinary sanctions.

Human review is required for consequential institutional decisions.

---

# Documentation Requirements

Each approved AI system should maintain documentation including:

- purpose;
- owner;
- approval authority;
- version;
- vendor information;
- training context;
- AI model;
- risk classification;
- review schedule;
- retirement plan.

Documentation supports governance and institutional continuity.

---

# Institutional AI Registry

SydTek maintains an AI Registry documenting every approved AI system.

Recommended fields include:

- AI identifier;
- system name;
- responsible owner;
- deployment status;
- educational purpose;
- risk classification;
- review date;
- current version.

The registry serves as the official inventory of institutional AI.

---

# Continuous Monitoring

Approved AI systems should be monitored for:

- accuracy;
- instructional quality;
- bias;
- reliability;
- learner satisfaction;
- faculty satisfaction;
- security;
- privacy compliance.

Monitoring results inform future improvements.

---

# Policy Review

The AI Governance Framework should be reviewed:

- annually;
- following significant technological advances;
- after major regulatory changes;
- after significant institutional incidents.

Policy revisions follow institutional governance procedures.

---

# Relationship to Accreditation

The framework supports accreditation by documenting:

- governance;
- faculty oversight;
- quality assurance;
- continuous improvement;
- institutional accountability.

AI governance should strengthen rather than complicate accreditation efforts.
