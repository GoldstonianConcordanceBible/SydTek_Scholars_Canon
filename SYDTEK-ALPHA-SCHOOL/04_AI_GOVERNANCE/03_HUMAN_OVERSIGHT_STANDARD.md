# Human Oversight Standard for Artificial Intelligence

**Repository:** SydTek Alpha School  
**Section:** Artificial Intelligence Governance  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** Canonical Institutional Governance Standard

---

# Purpose

The Human Oversight Standard establishes the institutional requirements for human supervision of Artificial Intelligence systems operating within SydTek Alpha School and SydTek University.

Artificial Intelligence may enhance learning, improve operational efficiency, and support institutional decision-making.

Artificial Intelligence does not replace institutional accountability.

Qualified human oversight remains required for all consequential educational and operational decisions.

---

# Governance Philosophy

SydTek follows a **Human-Governed, AI-Augmented** institutional model.

This model recognizes that AI systems can:

- analyze information;
- organize knowledge;
- generate recommendations;
- identify patterns;
- automate routine workflows.

Institutional authority remains vested in qualified human decision-makers.

---

# Guiding Principles

Human oversight should be:

- meaningful;
- documented;
- timely;
- proportional to institutional risk;
- transparent;
- accountable;
- evidence based.

Oversight should improve educational quality while preserving operational efficiency.

---

# Human Oversight Framework

```text
Institutional Policy

        │

        ▼

Faculty & Administrative Authority

        │

        ▼

Artificial Intelligence

        │

        ▼

Recommendation

        │

        ▼

Human Review

        │

        ▼

Institutional Decision
```

AI recommendations support—but do not replace—human judgment.

---

# Oversight Responsibilities

Qualified human reviewers remain responsible for:

- admissions decisions;
- competency verification;
- grade approval;
- graduation certification;
- faculty evaluation;
- curriculum approval;
- disciplinary actions;
- research approvals;
- accessibility accommodations;
- institutional policy implementation.

Institutional responsibility may not be delegated to AI.

---

# Faculty Oversight

Faculty remain responsible for:

- instructional quality;
- learning outcomes;
- competency verification;
- assessment integrity;
- learner feedback;
- academic mentoring.

AI may assist faculty with instructional preparation and feedback generation.

Faculty approve final academic decisions.

---

# Administrative Oversight

Administrators oversee AI systems supporting:

- enrollment management;
- scheduling;
- communications;
- budgeting;
- institutional analytics;
- operational planning.

Administrative leaders remain accountable for institutional outcomes.

---

# Research Oversight

Researchers using AI remain responsible for:

- research design;
- methodological rigor;
- data interpretation;
- citation practices;
- publication integrity;
- ethical conduct.

AI-generated research outputs should be reviewed before scholarly dissemination.

---

# Learner Oversight

Learners remain responsible for:

- submitted work;
- academic integrity;
- appropriate AI use;
- verification of AI-generated content.

Use of AI does not transfer responsibility for academic performance.

---

# Human-in-the-Loop

Certain institutional processes require human review before action.

Examples include:

- admissions recommendations;
- competency recognition;
- financial aid recommendations;
- academic progression;
- graduation approval.

AI may recommend.

Humans decide.

---

# Human-on-the-Loop

Some operational systems may function autonomously while remaining subject to periodic human monitoring.

Examples include:

- scheduling optimization;
- resource allocation suggestions;
- routine notifications;
- administrative workflow automation.

Personnel should be able to intervene when necessary.

---

# Human-in-Command

Institutional leadership remains responsible for:

- AI policy;
- institutional governance;
- strategic priorities;
- risk management;
- regulatory compliance.

Ultimate authority always resides with institutional leadership.

---

# Documentation

Human oversight activities should be documented.

Examples include:

- reviewer;
- review date;
- AI system involved;
- recommendation received;
- decision made;
- rationale;
- follow-up actions.

Documentation supports accountability and continuous improvement.

---

# Escalation

AI systems should escalate issues requiring human attention.

Examples include:

- suspected academic misconduct;
- learner safety concerns;
- accessibility requests;
- discrimination complaints;
- grading disputes;
- technical failures;
- legal inquiries.

Escalation pathways should be clearly documented.

---

# Training

Personnel responsible for AI oversight should receive training appropriate to their responsibilities.

Training may include:

- AI capabilities;
- AI limitations;
- prompt literacy;
- verification practices;
- privacy;
- accessibility;
- institutional policies.

Training should be reviewed periodically.

---

# Performance Monitoring

Institutional oversight effectiveness may be evaluated using:

- learner outcomes;
- faculty satisfaction;
- AI accuracy;
- intervention frequency;
- incident reports;
- audit findings;
- response times.

Monitoring supports evidence-based improvements.

---

# Relationship to Risk Classification

Oversight requirements increase with institutional risk.

- Minimal-risk AI may require periodic review.
- Moderate-risk AI requires operational oversight.
- High-risk AI requires documented human review before implementation.
- Critical-risk AI requires direct human decision-making.

Oversight remains proportional to potential institutional impact.

---

# Relationship to Educational Datasets

Human oversight ensures that AI systems continue to:

- retrieve approved instructional content;
- reinforce competency frameworks;
- respect curriculum boundaries;
- maintain assessment integrity.

Educational datasets remain the authoritative instructional source.

---

# Relationship to Accreditation

Human oversight demonstrates:

- faculty governance;
- institutional accountability;
- quality assurance;
- responsible AI implementation.

These practices support accreditation readiness and institutional credibility.

---

# Relationship to the SydTek Canon

The Human Oversight Standard implements:

- AI Governance Framework;
- AI Risk Classification Standard;
- Educational Dataset Standards;
- Competency Framework;
- Institutional Governance Principles.

Human oversight remains the defining characteristic of AI governance within SydTek.
