**Repository:** SydTek Alpha School

**Status:** Canonical Institutional Security Standard

---

# Purpose

The Artificial Intelligence Security and Privacy Standard establishes the institutional framework for protecting educational information, AI infrastructure, institutional knowledge repositories, and learner privacy throughout SydTek Scholars Alpha School and SydTek University.

Security and privacy are foundational institutional responsibilities that support educational quality, operational resilience, and public trust.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Educational Artificial Intelligence should be:

- secure by design;
- privacy conscious by default;
- governed throughout its lifecycle;
- monitored continuously;
- resilient against misuse.

Security and privacy should be integrated into planning, development, deployment, and retirement.

---

# Guiding Principles

Institutional AI security should be:

- risk based;
- proportionate;
- documented;
- continuously monitored;
- auditable;
- resilient;
- learner centered.

Privacy protections should support educational objectives while respecting applicable legal and institutional requirements.

# Security Objectives

Institutional security seeks to protect:

- educational datasets;
- AI Context Datasets;
- institutional repositories;
- operational systems;
- learner records;
- research materials;
- infrastructure;
- institutional reputation.

Protection strategies should be proportional to institutional risk.

---

# Identity Management

Institutional systems should maintain documented identities for:

- learners;
- faculty;
- staff;
- administrators;
- service accounts;
- approved AI systems.

Identity records support accountability and auditability.

---

# Authentication

Authentication mechanisms may include:

- passwords;
- multi-factor authentication;
- institutional single sign-on;
- hardware security keys;
- federated identity solutions.

Authentication strength should reflect system sensitivity.

---

# Authorization

Authorization should follow the principle of least privilege.

Access should be granted according to documented institutional roles.

Example roles include:

- learner;
- faculty;
- instructional designer;
- department chair;
- administrator;
- AI governance reviewer;
- system administrator.

Permissions should be reviewed periodically.

---

# Repository Protection

Educational repositories should protect:

- unpublished curriculum;
- assessment materials;
- answer keys;
- AI Context Datasets;
- institutional documentation.

Repository access should align with institutional governance policies.

---

# Privacy Principles

Institutional AI should:

- collect only information necessary for approved educational purposes;
- limit access to authorized personnel and systems;
- support secure storage and transmission where appropriate;
- define retention and disposal practices;
- document authorized data uses.

Privacy practices should comply with applicable laws and institutional policies.

---

# Educational Records

Protected learner information should be managed separately from instructional repositories.

Educational datasets are instructional resources.

Learner records are institutional records.

These resources should not be combined without documented authorization and appropriate safeguards.

---

# AI Data Handling

AI systems should clearly document:

- data sources;
- retrieval methods;
- storage practices;
- retention schedules;
- deletion procedures;
- logging practices.

Data governance supports transparency and accountability.

---

# Logging and Monitoring

Institutional logging may include:

- authentication events;
- authorization events;
- administrative actions;
- repository access;
- AI deployment events;
- security incidents.

Logs should be protected from unauthorized modification.

---

# Incident Response

Institutions should maintain documented procedures for responding to AI-related security or privacy incidents.

Response activities may include:

- incident identification;
- containment;
- investigation;
- recovery;
- communication;
- corrective actions;
- post-incident review.

Incident response plans should be reviewed periodically.

---

# Vendor Security

Third-party AI providers should be evaluated before institutional deployment.

Review considerations may include:

- security practices;
- privacy commitments;
- service reliability;
- contractual obligations;
- compliance documentation;
- support capabilities.

Vendor review supports institutional risk management.

---

# Security Testing

Periodic testing may include:

- vulnerability assessments;
- penetration testing where appropriate;
- access reviews;
- configuration reviews;
- backup verification;
- disaster recovery exercises.

Testing frequency should reflect institutional risk.

---

# Business Continuity

Institutions should maintain continuity plans addressing:

- AI service disruptions;
- repository recovery;
- infrastructure failures;
- cybersecurity incidents;
- disaster recovery.

Educational continuity should remain a priority.

---

# Security Training

Personnel should receive role-appropriate training regarding:

- AI security;
- privacy responsibilities;
- institutional policies;
- incident reporting;
- secure system use.

Training should be updated as technologies and institutional practices evolve.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Security and privacy practices improve through:

- audits;
- monitoring;
- faculty recommendations;
- learner feedback;
- technological advances;
- governance reviews.

Continuous improvement strengthens institutional resilience.

---

# Relationship to Accreditation

Security and privacy documentation supports accreditation by demonstrating:

- institutional governance;
- operational controls;
- responsible technology management;
- protection of educational resources.

Documentation should be retained according to institutional policies.

---

# Relationship to the SydTek Canon

The Artificial Intelligence Security and Privacy Standard implements:

- AI Governance Framework;
- AI Risk Classification Standard;
- Human Oversight Standard;
- Educational Dataset Standards;
- Institutional Security Principles.

Security and privacy enable trustworthy AI-supported education while protecting institutional assets and learner information.
