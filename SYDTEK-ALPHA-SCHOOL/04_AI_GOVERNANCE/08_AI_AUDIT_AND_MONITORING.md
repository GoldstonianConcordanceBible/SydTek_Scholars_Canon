# Artificial Intelligence Audit and Continuous Monitoring Standard

**Repository:** SydTek Alpha School  
**Section:** Artificial Intelligence Governance  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** Canonical Institutional Assurance Standard

---

# Purpose

The Artificial Intelligence Audit and Continuous Monitoring Standard establishes the institutional framework for evaluating, monitoring, auditing, and continuously improving AI systems deployed throughout SydTek Scholars Alpha School and SydTek University.

Continuous oversight ensures that AI systems remain:

- educationally effective;
- technically reliable;
- ethically governed;
- institutionally aligned;
- privacy conscious;
- secure;
- accessible;
- accreditation ready.

Monitoring is an ongoing institutional responsibility.

---

# Governance Philosophy

AI governance does not end at deployment.

Every approved AI system should be:

- monitored;
- evaluated;
- documented;
- periodically reviewed;
- improved through evidence.

Institutional trust depends upon continuous oversight rather than one-time approval.

---

# Guiding Principles

Audit activities should be:

- independent where appropriate;
- evidence based;
- documented;
- repeatable;
- transparent;
- risk proportional;
- improvement focused.

Audits are intended to strengthen institutional quality rather than assign blame.

# Monitoring Objectives

Institutional monitoring seeks to evaluate:

- instructional quality;
- response accuracy;
- learner outcomes;
- faculty satisfaction;
- system reliability;
- accessibility;
- privacy compliance;
- security;
- governance compliance.

Monitoring should support educational excellence.

---

# Operational Monitoring

Operational monitoring may include:

- uptime;
- response latency;
- availability;
- retrieval success;
- infrastructure performance;
- system utilization.

Technical monitoring supports dependable educational services.

---

# Instructional Monitoring

Educational review evaluates:

- competency alignment;
- learning outcome support;
- instructional consistency;
- curriculum alignment;
- educational dataset retrieval quality.

Faculty participation strengthens instructional quality.

---

# AI Response Quality

Response evaluation may consider:

- factual accuracy;
- instructional clarity;
- relevance;
- completeness;
- consistency;
- hallucination frequency;
- citation quality where applicable.

Quality metrics should be documented.

---

# Learner Outcome Monitoring

Institutional evidence may include:

- competency attainment;
- assessment performance;
- learner engagement;
- progression;
- retention;
- learner satisfaction.

AI effectiveness should be evaluated through educational outcomes rather than usage alone.

---

# Faculty Feedback

Faculty should have opportunities to report:

- instructional concerns;
- inaccurate responses;
- curriculum misalignment;
- AI improvement recommendations;
- accessibility observations.

Faculty expertise remains central to educational quality.

---

# Privacy Monitoring

Monitoring should verify compliance with institutional privacy requirements.

Examples include:

- access controls;
- authorized data use;
- audit logging;
- retention practices;
- incident reporting.

Privacy reviews should occur periodically.

---

# Security Monitoring

Security monitoring may evaluate:

- authentication;
- authorization;
- intrusion detection;
- vulnerability management;
- system updates;
- incident response.

Security controls should remain proportional to institutional risk.

---

# Accessibility Monitoring

Accessibility reviews evaluate:

- learner usability;
- assistive technology compatibility;
- multilingual functionality where applicable;
- interface accessibility;
- instructional accessibility.

Accessibility improvements should follow documented review processes.

---

# Governance Compliance

Institutional governance reviews verify compliance with:

- AI Governance Framework;
- Human Oversight Standard;
- Risk Classification Standard;
- Ethics Standard;
- Educational Dataset Standards.

Governance reviews ensure AI systems remain institutionally aligned.

---

# Audit Schedule

Recommended review frequencies:

| AI System Type | Suggested Review |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Low-Risk Administrative AI | Annual |
| AI Tutoring Systems | Every 6 months |
| Digital Twins | Every 6 months |
| High-Risk Institutional AI | Quarterly |
| Critical AI Systems | Continuous monitoring with formal quarterly review |

Institutions may adjust schedules according to documented risk.

---

# Corrective Actions

When issues are identified, institutions should document:

- findings;
- severity;
- root cause;
- corrective actions;
- responsible personnel;
- completion date;
- follow-up review.

Corrective actions should be tracked until resolved.

---

# AI Registry Updates

Audit results should update the institutional AI Registry.

Registry updates may include:

- review date;
- risk classification;
- approval status;
- corrective actions;
- current version;
- next scheduled review.

The registry provides a current institutional inventory of AI systems.

Continuous improvement is evidence driven.
