# Purpose

Artificial Intelligence is a core capability of SydTek Alpha School.

However, AI does not govern the institution.

People govern the institution.

AI augments educational delivery, administrative efficiency, research, learner support, and institutional decision support while remaining subject to documented human oversight, academic governance, legal compliance, and institutional accountability.

This section establishes the governance framework for every AI system operating within:

- SydTek Scholars Alpha School;
- SydTek University;
- Educational Dataset repositories;
- institutional operations;
- learner services;
- faculty services;
- research systems;
- institutional licensing.

---

# Vision

SydTek seeks to become an AI-native educational institution.

An AI-native institution does not simply purchase AI software.

Instead, AI is intentionally designed into:

- curriculum;
- educational datasets;
- tutoring;
- faculty support;
- institutional analytics;
- governance;
- research;
- licensing;
- continuous improvement.

AI should expand educational opportunity while preserving academic integrity and human dignity.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Artificial Intelligence should operate according to five institutional principles.

1. Human authority remains final.

2. AI supports rather than replaces qualified educators.

3. Every consequential decision requires appropriate human oversight.

4. AI systems must remain transparent, auditable, and version controlled.

5. Learners remain the beneficiaries—not the products—of educational AI.

# AI Categories

SydTek recognizes multiple categories of AI.

## Instructional AI

Supports:

- tutoring;
- explanations;
- study planning;
- adaptive learning;
- competency development.

---

## Faculty AI

Supports:

- lesson planning;
- rubric development;
- feedback drafting;
- curriculum mapping;
- instructional analytics.

Faculty remain responsible for all instructional decisions.

---

## Administrative AI

Supports:

- admissions;
- scheduling;
- document preparation;
- communications;
- workflow automation.

Administrative AI should improve operational efficiency.

---

## Research AI

Supports:

- literature review;
- research organization;
- coding assistance;
- data interpretation;
- scholarly productivity.

Researchers remain responsible for methodological rigor and scholarly integrity.

---

## Institutional AI

Supports:

- dashboards;
- forecasting;
- budgeting;
- institutional effectiveness;
- quality assurance;
- accreditation reporting.

Institutional AI informs decisions but does not independently establish institutional policy.

# AI Governance Principles

Every approved AI system should be:

- documented;
- reviewed;
- monitored;
- explainable where appropriate;
- secure;
- privacy preserving;
- regularly updated;
- academically aligned.

AI systems should never operate outside documented institutional governance.

---

# Human Oversight

Human review is required for consequential decisions involving:

- admissions;
- grade assignment;
- competency certification;
- graduation;
- disciplinary action;
- employment;
- accessibility accommodations;
- research approval.

AI recommendations remain advisory unless institutional policy explicitly defines otherwise for low-risk administrative tasks.

---

# Educational Alignment

Every instructional AI system should align with:

- Educational Dataset Standards;
- Competency Framework;
- Learning Outcomes;
- Assessment Standards;
- Faculty Governance.

AI should reinforce—not redefine—the curriculum.

---

# Transparency

Learners and faculty should know when AI is being used.

Institutional disclosures should identify:

- AI-assisted services;
- limitations;
- human oversight;
- privacy protections;
- acceptable use.

Transparency strengthens institutional trust.

---
