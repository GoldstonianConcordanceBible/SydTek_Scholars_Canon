# Artificial Intelligence Tutoring Standard

**Repository:** SydTek Alpha School  
**Section:** Artificial Intelligence Governance  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** Canonical Academic Standard

---

# Purpose

The Artificial Intelligence Tutoring Standard establishes how AI tutoring systems are designed, deployed, monitored, and governed throughout SydTek Scholars Alpha School and SydTek University.

AI tutoring is intended to expand educational access, improve learner outcomes, personalize instruction, and support faculty.

AI tutoring supplements human instruction.

It does not replace teachers, professors, advisors, or mentors.

---

# Educational Philosophy

Every learner progresses differently.

Artificial Intelligence enables instruction that adapts to individual learning needs while remaining aligned with institutional competencies and faculty-approved curriculum.

AI tutors should:

- encourage understanding;
- promote critical thinking;
- reinforce competencies;
- identify misconceptions;
- recommend appropriate learning resources.

AI tutoring should not encourage shortcut learning or academic dishonesty.

---

# Guiding Principles

Every AI tutoring system should be:

- learner centered;
- faculty governed;
- competency aligned;
- transparent;
- accessible;
- explainable where appropriate;
- privacy preserving;
- continuously improving.

Instructional quality remains the highest priority.

---

# AI Tutoring Architecture

```text
Learner

        │

        ▼

Learner Question

        │

        ▼

AI Context Dataset (RAG)

        │

        ▼

Foundation Language Model

        │

        ▼

Faculty-Approved Response

        │

        ▼

Learner
```

Educational datasets remain the primary instructional authority.

The AI tutor retrieves approved instructional context before generating responses.

---

# Core Tutoring Functions

AI tutors may provide:

- concept explanations;
- guided practice;
- adaptive questioning;
- study planning;
- prerequisite review;
- formative feedback;
- learning resource recommendations;
- competency progress summaries.

Tutoring interactions should reinforce official curriculum.

---

# Personalized Learning

AI tutoring may adapt to:

- learner progress;
- demonstrated competencies;
- assessment history;
- preferred pacing;
- accessibility needs;
- educational level.

Personalization should not reduce academic expectations.

---

# Competency Alignment

Every tutoring interaction should reinforce:

- institutional competencies;
- program outcomes;
- course outcomes;
- educational datasets;
- assessment expectations.

Competency development remains the objective of tutoring.

---

# Faculty Authority

Faculty determine:

- curriculum;
- learning outcomes;
- competency standards;
- assessment expectations;
- acceptable instructional boundaries.

AI tutors implement—not redefine—faculty guidance.

---

# Feedback Standards

AI-generated feedback should be:

- constructive;
- specific;
- educational;
- respectful;
- actionable.

Feedback should guide learners toward improvement rather than simply identifying errors.

---

# Academic Integrity

AI tutors should support ethical learning.

Tutors should:

- explain concepts;
- encourage original work;
- recommend study strategies;
- provide formative guidance.

Tutors should not:

- complete graded assignments on behalf of learners;
- fabricate citations;
- generate fraudulent academic work;
- assist with academic misconduct.

Institutional academic integrity policies remain applicable.

---

# Accessibility

AI tutoring systems should support:

- multiple languages where available;
- screen readers;
- captions;
- text alternatives;
- adaptable interfaces;
- accessible instructional resources.

Accessibility should be considered during design and periodic review.

---

# Escalation

AI tutors should recommend human assistance when appropriate.

Examples include:

- repeated learner confusion;
- accessibility requests;
- mental health concerns;
- academic misconduct;
- grading disputes;
- advising questions;
- technical support.

Escalation pathways should direct learners to appropriate institutional resources.

---

# Privacy

AI tutoring systems should minimize collection of personal information.

Institutional AI tutors should not disclose protected learner information without appropriate authorization.

Instructional context remains separate from protected education records.

---

# Quality Assurance

AI tutoring quality is evaluated using:

- learner satisfaction;
- faculty feedback;
- competency attainment;
- response accuracy;
- hallucination frequency;
- accessibility performance;
- instructional consistency.

Quality improvements should be incorporated through documented version-control processes.

---

# Human Oversight

Faculty remain responsible for:

- curriculum quality;
- instructional expectations;
- competency verification;
- assessment standards.

Human review is required for consequential academic decisions.

AI tutoring remains advisory.

---

# Analytics

Institutional analytics may evaluate:

- tutoring utilization;
- learning progression;
- competency development;
- frequently missed concepts;
- intervention effectiveness.

Analytics should support educational improvement while respecting applicable privacy requirements.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Tutoring systems should improve through:

- educational dataset updates;
- AI Context Dataset revisions;
- faculty review;
- learner feedback;
- instructional analytics;
- accreditation recommendations.

Continuous improvement strengthens instructional quality over time.

---

# Relationship to Educational Datasets

AI tutoring retrieves instructional guidance from:

- Educational Datasets;
- AI Context Datasets;
- Competency Frameworks;
- Learning Outcomes;
- Assessment Standards.

Educational datasets remain the authoritative instructional source.

---

# Relationship to the SydTek Canon

The AI Tutoring Standard implements:

- AI Governance Framework;
- Human Oversight Standard;
- Educational Dataset Standards;
- Competency Framework;
- Personalized Learning Pathways.

AI tutoring expands educational opportunity while preserving faculty authority and institutional academic standards.
