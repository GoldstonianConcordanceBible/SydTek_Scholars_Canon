# Artificial Intelligence Ethics Standard

**Repository:** SydTek Alpha School  
**Section:** Artificial Intelligence Governance  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** Canonical Institutional Ethics Standard

---

# Purpose

The Artificial Intelligence Ethics Standard establishes the ethical principles governing the design, acquisition, deployment, operation, monitoring, and retirement of AI systems throughout SydTek Scholars Alpha School and SydTek University.

Ethical governance protects learners, faculty, staff, institutional partners, and the broader public while encouraging responsible educational innovation.

Ethics should be integrated into every stage of the AI lifecycle rather than addressed only after deployment.

---

# Ethical Philosophy

Artificial Intelligence exists to serve educational purposes.

Technology should strengthen:

- learning;
- teaching;
- research;
- accessibility;
- institutional effectiveness;
- human flourishing.

AI should never undermine academic integrity, learner autonomy, or institutional accountability.

---

# Guiding Ethical Principles

Every institutional AI system should demonstrate:

1. Human dignity
2. Learner-centered design
3. Academic integrity
4. Transparency
5. Accountability
6. Fairness
7. Privacy
8. Accessibility
9. Security
10. Continuous improvement

These principles apply across instructional, administrative, research, and operational AI systems.

---

# Respect for Human Dignity

Every learner should be treated with dignity and respect.

AI systems should:

- communicate respectfully;
- avoid degrading language;
- support learner growth;
- encourage independent thinking;
- recognize learner agency.

AI should not manipulate, coerce, or intentionally deceive learners.

---

# Learner-Centered Design

Educational AI should prioritize learner success.

Systems should support:

- understanding;
- critical thinking;
- curiosity;
- lifelong learning;
- educational opportunity.

Learners should remain active participants rather than passive recipients of AI-generated content.

---

# Academic Integrity

AI systems should reinforce institutional academic integrity policies.

Examples include:

- encouraging original work;
- explaining concepts;
- supporting proper citation;
- discouraging plagiarism;
- discouraging fabrication.

Institutional academic standards apply regardless of AI assistance.

---

# Transparency

Learners and faculty should understand:

- when AI is being used;
- what AI can do;
- AI limitations;
- when human oversight applies.

Institutional transparency strengthens trust.

---

# Accountability

Institutional accountability remains human.

Personnel responsible for AI systems should understand:

- system purpose;
- operational limitations;
- governance requirements;
- reporting procedures.

AI systems do not assume legal or institutional responsibility.

---

# Fairness

Institutions should seek to identify and reduce unfair outcomes where reasonably practicable.

Review may include:

- instructional quality;
- accessibility;
- evaluation consistency;
- representative testing;
- governance oversight.

Fairness evaluations should be documented and periodically reviewed.

---

# Privacy

AI systems should minimize collection and use of personal information.

Institutional AI should:

- protect learner records;
- separate instructional datasets from protected information;
- support secure processing;
- comply with applicable institutional policies and legal requirements.

Privacy considerations should be incorporated during system design.

---

# Accessibility

Educational AI should support broad learner participation.

Accessibility considerations include:

- assistive technologies;
- screen readers;
- captions;
- multilingual support where available;
- adaptable interfaces;
- accessible instructional resources.

Accessibility should be reviewed throughout the AI lifecycle.

---

# Security

Responsible AI requires appropriate technical safeguards.

Security practices may include:

- authentication;
- authorization;
- encryption where appropriate;
- audit logging;
- monitoring;
- incident response.

Security controls should be proportional to institutional risk.

---

# Human Oversight

Consequential educational decisions require human review.

Examples include:

- competency verification;
- grade approval;
- graduation certification;
- disciplinary actions;
- admissions decisions.

AI recommendations remain advisory unless institutional policy explicitly authorizes limited automation for low-risk administrative functions.

---

# Ethical Review

Significant AI implementations should receive ethical review before deployment.

Review may consider:

- learner impact;
- institutional impact;
- privacy;
- accessibility;
- fairness;
- transparency;
- security;
- educational benefit.

Ethical review should be documented.

---

# AI Use by Learners

Learners should use AI responsibly.

Appropriate uses may include:

- studying;
- concept clarification;
- brainstorming;
- revision;
- practice.

Learners remain responsible for submitted work and compliance with academic integrity policies.

---

# AI Use by Faculty

Faculty may appropriately use AI to support:

- lesson planning;
- curriculum development;
- instructional design;
- formative feedback;
- educational resource creation.

Faculty remain responsible for academic quality and instructional decisions.

---

# Research Ethics

Researchers using AI should:

- disclose AI use where appropriate;
- verify outputs;
- cite sources accurately;
- preserve methodological integrity;
- comply with applicable research ethics policies.

AI does not replace scholarly judgment.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Ethical governance improves through:

- learner feedback;
- faculty recommendations;
- institutional audits;
- governance reviews;
- technological advances;
- evolving educational practices.

Ethics should remain an ongoing institutional conversation.

---

# Relationship to Educational Datasets

Educational datasets support ethical AI by providing:

- authoritative instructional content;
- competency alignment;
- faculty-approved explanations;
- instructional boundaries.

Repository quality directly influences ethical AI performance.

---

# Relationship to the SydTek Canon

The Artificial Intelligence Ethics Standard implements:

- AI Governance Framework;
- Human Oversight Standard;
- AI Risk Classification Standard;
- Educational Dataset Standards;
- Institutional Governance Principles.

Ethical AI supports SydTek's mission by advancing educational opportunity while preserving human dignity, academic integrity, and institutional accountability.
