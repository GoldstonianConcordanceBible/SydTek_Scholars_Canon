PURPOSE

Establish the transfer credit policy for
students entering SydTek Scholars from
homeschool programs, private schools,
public schools, online programs,
microcredential systems, and portfolio-based
learning environments.

⸻

TRANSFER CREDIT MODEL

SydTek Scholars may evaluate prior learning through:
Official Transcript
Parent Portfolio
Course Completion Records
Seat-Time Records
Assessment Evidence
Microcredential Records
Faculty Review
Registrar Approval

⸻

Homeschool Records
Private School Transcripts

Online School Transcripts
Accredited Course Providers
Portfolio-Based Learning
Microcredentials
Dual Enrollment Records
Language Study Records
Service Learning Records

Student Name

⸻

CREDIT REVIEW PROCESS

Step 1
Parent or school submits records.
Step 2
Registrar reviews documentation.
Step 3
Academic Director reviews academic fit.
Step 4
Chief Compliance Officer reviews record completeness.
Step 5
Faculty reviewer may evaluate portfolio evidence.
Step 6
Registrar records approved transfer credit.

⸻

HIGH SCHOOL TRANSFER CREDIT

Grades 9-12 transfer credits must map to:
English / Literature
Mathematics
Science
Social Studies
Spanish / World Language
Bible / Covenant Studies
AI / Digital Literacy
Health / Wellness
Arts / Media / Music
Service Learning
Portfolio / Capstone

⸻

SPANISH TRANSFER CREDIT

Spanish transfer credit may be granted through:
Prior Spanish Course
Language Portfolio
Oral Assessment
Writing Sample
Vocabulary Evidence
Transcripted Spanish Credit
Faculty Review

⸻

MAXIMUM TRANSFER CREDIT

Grades 9-12
Up to 75% of graduation credits may be accepted
through transfer review.
Minimum SydTek Completion Requirement
25% of high school credits must be completed
through SydTek Scholars curriculum, portfolio,
or faculty-reviewed evidence.

⸻

PORTFOLIO-BASED CREDIT

Portfolio-based credit may be awarded when
student work demonstrates:
Learning Outcomes
Seat-Time Equivalent
Assessment Evidence
Parent Verification
Faculty Review
Registrar Approval

⸻

CREDIT DENIAL CONDITIONS

Credit may be denied if:
Records are incomplete.
Course content does not match grade level.
Seat-time is not documented.
Portfolio evidence is insufficient.
Work cannot be verified.
Course does not align with graduation requirements.

⸻

APPEAL PROCESS

Parent or guardian may appeal a credit decision.
Appeal must include additional evidence.
Registrar reviews appeal.
Academic Director reviews academic fit.
Chief Compliance Officer reviews procedure.
Final decision is documented in student record.
