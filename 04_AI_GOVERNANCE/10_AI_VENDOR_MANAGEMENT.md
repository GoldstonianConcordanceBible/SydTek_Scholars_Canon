# Artificial Intelligence Vendor Management Standard

**Repository:** SydTek Alpha School

**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** Canonical Institutional Procurement Standard

---

# Purpose

The Artificial Intelligence Vendor Management Standard establishes how SydTek Alpha School evaluates, procures, deploys, manages, reviews, and retires third-party Artificial Intelligence technologies.

The institution recognizes that educational AI may include:

- commercial AI platforms;
- open-source AI models;
- hosted AI services;
- enterprise AI systems;
- educational AI vendors;
- cloud infrastructure providers.

Vendor governance protects instructional quality, institutional independence, learner privacy, and long-term sustainability.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Technology vendors support SydTek.

Technology vendors do not define SydTek.

Institutional governance, curriculum, competencies, educational datasets, and academic standards remain under SydTek control regardless of the technology provider.

Vendor relationships should strengthen institutional capability without creating unnecessary dependence.

---

# Guiding Principles

Vendor management should be:

- transparent;
- competitive;
- evidence based;
- educationally aligned;
- privacy conscious;
- financially sustainable;
- technically interoperable;
- periodically reviewed.

Technology decisions should support the institution's mission.

---

# Evaluation Criteria

Potential vendors should be evaluated using multiple dimensions.

Examples include:

- educational effectiveness;
- instructional alignment;
- AI capabilities;
- security;
- privacy;
- accessibility;
- interoperability;
- reliability;
- scalability;
- financial stability;
- customer support;
- total cost of ownership.

Evaluation criteria should be documented before procurement decisions.

---

# Educational Alignment

AI vendors should demonstrate compatibility with:

- Educational Dataset Standards;
- Competency Framework;
- Learning Outcomes;
- Personalized Learning Pathways;
- AI Context Datasets.

Vendor technology should adapt to institutional curriculum—not require the curriculum to adapt to the vendor.

---

# Technical Interoperability

Preferred vendors support:

- APIs;
- standards-based integrations;
- data portability;
- repository synchronization;
- identity management;
- audit logging.

Interoperability reduces vendor lock-in and supports long-term flexibility.

---

# Security Review

Vendor evaluations should include review of:

- authentication;
- authorization;
- encryption practices;
- infrastructure security;
- vulnerability management;
- incident response;
- independent security assessments where available.

Security documentation should be maintained as part of procurement records.

---

# Privacy Review

Privacy evaluation should consider:

- data collection;
- data retention;
- data deletion;
- data ownership;
- subprocessors;
- contractual commitments;
- cross-border data handling where applicable.

Institutional privacy expectations should be reflected in vendor agreements.

---

# Accessibility Review

AI vendors should support accessible educational experiences.

Evaluation may include:

- assistive technology compatibility;
- caption support;
- keyboard navigation;
- multilingual capabilities where applicable;
- accessibility documentation.

Accessibility should remain an institutional priority.

---

# Pilot Deployment

Before institution-wide adoption, vendors should participate in a documented pilot when appropriate.

Pilot evaluations may consider:

- instructional quality;
- faculty usability;
- learner satisfaction;
- operational reliability;
- technical integration;
- support responsiveness.

Pilot findings inform final procurement decisions.

---

# Contract Management

Institutional agreements should clearly address:

- scope of services;
- pricing;
- renewal terms;
- service-level expectations;
- intellectual property;
- data ownership;
- termination rights;
- support responsibilities.

Contracts should support institutional flexibility.

---

# Vendor Performance

Approved vendors should be evaluated periodically using:

- service availability;
- instructional effectiveness;
- support quality;
- security performance;
- privacy compliance;
- faculty feedback;
- learner feedback.

Performance reviews support evidence-based renewal decisions.

---

# Exit Strategy

Every vendor relationship should include an exit strategy.

Planning may address:

- data export;
- repository migration;
- instructional continuity;
- contract termination;
- replacement technologies.

Exit planning reduces operational disruption.

---

# Institutional Independence

SydTek retains ownership of:

- educational datasets;
- competency frameworks;
- curriculum;
- institutional governance;
- learning outcomes;
- academic standards.

Vendor platforms should not become the institution's sole repository of educational knowledge.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Vendor management improves through:

- procurement reviews;
- faculty recommendations;
- learner feedback;
- governance audits;
- market analysis;
- technological advances.

Continuous review supports long-term institutional resilience.

---

# Relationship to Accreditation

Vendor documentation supports accreditation by demonstrating:

- governance;
- procurement oversight;
- technology management;
- educational quality;
- continuous improvement.

Vendor relationships should strengthen institutional credibility.

---

# Relationship to the SydTek Canon

The Artificial Intelligence Vendor Management Standard implements:

- AI Governance Framework;
- AI Security and Privacy Standard;
- AI Risk Classification Standard;
- Educational Dataset Standards;
- Institutional Procurement Principles.

Vendor governance ensures that SydTek remains academically independent while leveraging responsible AI innovation.
